## WHAT IS DATADOG?

**Model:** Cloud-native monitoring and analytics platform. Revenue comes from a usage-based SaaS model (pay per host, per GB ingested, per indexed span, etc.) across 20+ product modules. Customers pay based on consumption of infrastructure monitoring, APM, log management, security, and CI/CD visibility. Multi-product land-and-expand strategy: customers adopt one module and expand into others.

**Revenue:** $3.73B for fiscal year 2025. Q4 2025 revenue was $953M, up 29% YoY. Guidance for FY2026: $4.06B--$4.10B (18--20% growth). Bookings reached $1.63B in Q4 2025, up 37% YoY. Free cash flow margin of 31%. Gross margin 81.4%.

**Headcount:** ~8,100 employees as of December 31, 2025 (24.6% increase YoY), operating across 35 countries. Actively hiring across engineering, sales, and go-to-market functions.

**Tech Stack (from job postings):** Go, Python, Java, React, TypeScript, Redux, Kubernetes, Bazel. Multi-region, multi-cloud architecture.

**Position:** Market leader in cloud observability. Dominant in APM, infrastructure monitoring, and log management. Aggressively expanding into security (Cloud SIEM), CI/CD visibility, data observability (Metaplane acquisition), feature flagging/experimentation (Eppo acquisition), and autonomous QA testing (Propolis acquisition, January 2026). Competing against Splunk (Cisco), New Relic, Dynatrace, Elastic, and Grafana Labs.

## THE SPECIFIC PAIN

**There is no pain to exploit here -- for two separate reasons:**

1. **Amit Agarwal is no longer at Datadog.** He cannot buy DevPulse for Datadog. He is an investor at ICONIQ Capital. His "pain" is finding great enterprise software companies to invest in, not solving CI/CD pipeline bottlenecks.

2. **Datadog already has CI Visibility.** Datadog's CI Pipeline Visibility product directly competes with DevPulse. It offers pipeline performance monitoring, bottleneck identification (nodes ranked by execution time percentage), flame graph visualization, failure trend analysis, CI monitors with alertable thresholds, and integration with all major CI providers. This is a first-party product from a $4B revenue company with 81% gross margins and a platform consolidation strategy. Datadog is not a buyer of DevPulse -- it is a **competitor** in the CI/CD analytics space.
