# DataLens ABM Campaign Plan: Financial Services Enterprise Expansion

## Campaign Overview

**Objective:** Land platform-wide expansion deals across six named financial services accounts currently on the mid-market DataLens plan.

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Target Accounts | JPMorgan, Goldman Sachs, Citi, BNY Mellon, State Street, Northern Trust |
| Current Relationship | Existing customers, mid-market plan |
| Deal Size Target | $850K ARR average per account |
| Total Pipeline Target | $5.1M ARR (6 accounts x $850K) |
| Campaign Budget | $120,000 over 90 days |
| Campaign Duration | 90 days (3 phases of 30 days each) |
| Team | 1 Content Marketer, 1 Demand Gen Manager, 1 Shared Designer (12 hrs/wk), 4 Enterprise AEs |
| CRM | HubSpot |
| Product | DataLens -- Real-Time Data Observability Platform |

## 1. Account Intelligence & Segmentation

### 1.1 Account Tiering

Given all six are named enterprise financial services accounts, tier them by expansion potential and engagement velocity:

**Tier 1 -- Highest Expansion Probability (assign 2 AEs, 45% of budget = $54,000):**
- JPMorgan -- Largest data footprint, most complex infrastructure, highest ceiling
- Goldman Sachs -- Heavy investment in data infrastructure modernization, engineering-led culture

**Tier 2 -- Strong Expansion Probability (assign 1 AE, 35% of budget = $42,000):**
- Citi -- Global operations create multi-region observability needs
- BNY Mellon -- Custody and asset servicing = massive data pipeline complexity

**Tier 3 -- Steady Expansion Probability (assign 1 AE, 20% of budget = $24,000):**
- State Street -- Concentrated use cases in asset management data flows
- Northern Trust -- Smaller footprint but high-value wealth management data needs

For each account, the Demand Gen Manager and assigned AE must document:

| Research Dimension | What to Capture |
|---|---|
| Current DataLens usage | Which teams, use cases, data volume, features used, NPS/CSAT |
| Expansion whitespace | Adjacent teams, unused modules, additional data sources not monitored |
| Org chart & buying committee | Map 8-12 contacts per account across Economic Buyer, Champion, Technical Evaluator, End Users, Procurement |
| Strategic initiatives | Public filings, earnings calls, press releases mentioning data strategy, cloud migration, regulatory compliance, operational resilience |

| Renewal timeline | When does mid-market contract renew? Use this as expansion leverage |
| Pain signals | Support tickets, feature requests, CSM notes indicating unmet needs |

**HubSpot Action:** Create a custom property "ABM Tier" on each company record (Tier 1 / Tier 2 / Tier 3). Create a custom property "Expansion Whitespace" as a multi-select with values: New Teams, New Regions, New Data Sources, New Modules, Platform-Wide License. Tag all six accounts.

## 7. Messaging Framework

### 7.1 Core Narrative

**Theme:** "From Team-Level Observability to Enterprise Data Trust"

**Positioning:** DataLens is the platform that financial services institutions rely on to ensure every data pipeline, every data product, and every regulatory report is trustworthy -- in real time, at enterprise scale.

**Key Message Pillars:**

| Pillar | Message | Proof Point |
|---|---|---|
| Enterprise Scale | "You've proven DataLens works for one team. Imagine that confidence across every data pipeline in the firm." | Current usage metrics + projected coverage |
| Regulatory Confidence | "Real-time observability is no longer optional -- regulators expect you to know the state of your data at all times. SOX, BCBS 239, DORA, OCC heightened standards all demand it." | Regulatory mapping document |
| Consolidation Value | "Replace 3-4 point solutions with one platform. Reduce vendor management overhead, unify alerting, get a single source of truth for data health." | TCO comparison model |
| Speed to Value | "Your team was live in weeks, not months. Enterprise rollout follows the same playbook -- we've done it at [reference customer]." | Implementation timeline benchmarks |
| Strategic Partnership | "DataLens isn't a tool purchase -- it's a strategic data infrastructure decision. We invest in our enterprise customers with dedicated support, custom roadmap input, and executive alignment." | Advisory board invite, dedicated CSM, SLA commitments |

### 7.2 Persona-Specific Messaging

**Economic Buyer (CTO/CDO):**
- Lead with: Business risk reduction, regulatory compliance, vendor consolidation ROI
- Avoid: Technical implementation details
- CTA: Executive briefing, peer dinner, reference call with peer CxO

**Champion (VP Data Engineering):**
- Lead with: Career impact of sponsoring enterprise rollout, platform roadmap alignment, expanded influence across teams
- Avoid: Over-selling; they already believe. Enable them to sell internally.
- CTA: Advisory board seat, roadmap preview, co-build the business case

**Technical Evaluator (Principal/Staff Engineer):**
- Lead with: Architecture scalability, API extensibility, integration with their stack (Kafka, Spark, Snowflake, Databricks, Airflow), multi-tenancy, RBAC
- Avoid: Marketing fluff, ROI claims without technical substance
- CTA: Technical workshop, architecture review, sandbox environment

**End Users (Data Engineers, SREs):**
- Lead with: Productivity gains, reduced incident response time, self-serve monitoring
- Avoid: Procurement/pricing discussions
- CTA: Training sessions, user community access, beta features

**Procurement/Vendor Management:**
- Lead with: Security posture, compliance certifications, SLA commitments, flexible contract structure
- Avoid: Product features -- they care about risk and terms
- CTA: Security review package, reference calls, contract discussion

## 12. Post-Campaign (Days 91-180)

The 90-day campaign is the opening offensive. Enterprise financial services deals of this size typically close in 90-180 days. Plan for continued effort:

1. **Deals in Negotiation:** Continue AE engagement. Use campaign momentum and materials. No incremental budget needed -- just sales effort.
2. **Stalled Deals:** Reassess account tier. Consider: (a) custom product roadmap commitment, (b) strategic partnership offer, (c) executive relationship deepening, (d) wait for contract renewal as natural forcing function.
3. **Closed-Won Deals:** Immediate warm handoff to implementation and CSM. Use as reference customers for remaining open deals. Update case studies.
4. **Closed-Lost/No Decision:** Document reasons. Feed back to product and marketing. Nurture contacts for future opportunity.
5. **Campaign Retrospective:** Full team review at Day 95. Document: what worked, what didn't, budget efficiency, content performance, channel ROI. Feed learnings into next ABM campaign.
