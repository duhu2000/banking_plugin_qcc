## STRATEGY: ACCOUNT-BASED MARKETING (ABM)

**Rationale:** Six named enterprise accounts with an $850K average deal target is a textbook ABM motion. These are existing customers on a mid-market plan -- meaning we already have internal champions, usage data, and product familiarity. The campaign should surround each account's buying committee with multi-channel, multi-contact, account-specific content that builds the business case for platform-wide expansion. ABM is the only strategy that justifies the per-account investment at this deal size.

**Campaign architecture:** 1:Few ABM (six accounts in one financial services cluster, with account-specific personalisation layered on top of shared vertical content).

---

## AUDIENCE DEFINITION

**Primary persona -- The Technical Champion (already a user):**
VP of Data Engineering / Head of Data Observability / Director of Platform Engineering. Currently sponsors the mid-market DataLens deployment. Cares about: data pipeline reliability, observability coverage gaps, incident response time, engineering team productivity. This person needs ammunition to build the internal business case for expansion.

**Secondary persona -- The Economic Buyer:**
CIO / CTO / CFO / SVP Technology. Approves the budget increase from mid-market to enterprise platform-wide. Cares about: total cost of ownership, risk reduction, regulatory compliance (critical in financial services), vendor consolidation, ROI vs. current tooling.

**Tertiary persona -- The Influencer/End User:**
Senior Data Engineers, SREs, Platform Engineers currently using DataLens on the mid-market plan. Their adoption data and satisfaction are proof points. Their frustration with current plan limitations is the expansion trigger.

**Addressable audience size:** 6 accounts x ~8-12 relevant contacts per account = 48-72 target contacts.

**Target reach:** 100% of identified contacts touched at least 3x across channels within 90 days.

## CHANNEL MIX WITH BUDGET ALLOCATION

### Phase 1: Account Intelligence & Foundation (Days 1-14) -- $12,000

**1. Account Research & Stakeholder Mapping -- $0 (staff time only)**
Purpose: Build the intelligence layer that powers every subsequent touchpoint.
Format: Per-account dossier -- org chart of data/technology leadership, current DataLens usage data (pull from product analytics), known pain points from CS/support tickets, upcoming initiatives (earnings calls, press releases, job postings signalling data infrastructure investment).
Target: Complete stakeholder map with 8-12 named contacts per account within 10 business days. Identify the expansion champion and the economic buyer at each account.

**2. HubSpot ABM Infrastructure Setup -- $0 (staff time only)**
Purpose: Configure CRM for campaign tracking and lead routing.
Format: Create target account lists in HubSpot, configure account-level scoring, build campaign attribution, set up deal stage properties for expansion pipeline.
Target: All 6 accounts tagged, all 48-72 contacts enriched, account scoring model live.

**3. Account-Specific Content Production -- $12,000**
Purpose: Create the core content assets that power the entire campaign.
Format:
- 1 Financial Services Data Observability Benchmark Report (gated) -- $5,000 (design, data visualisation, production)
- 6 Account-Specific "DataLens Impact Briefs" (1-pagers showing each account's current usage, value delivered, and expansion opportunity) -- $4,200 ($700 x 6)
- 1 ROI Calculator / Business Case Template customised for financial services -- $1,800 (design + interactive PDF)
- Email templates and LinkedIn content (produced by content marketer, designed by shared designer) -- $1,000 (design hours)
Target: All content assets production-ready by Day 14.

### Phase 2: Warm Engagement & Multi-Threading (Days 15-45) -- $48,000

**4. LinkedIn Sponsored Content (Account-Targeted) -- $18,000 ($600/day x 30 days)**
Purpose: Surround target accounts with credibility content. Financial services data leaders at these six companies see DataLens as the category leader in their feed.
Format: LinkedIn company-targeted ads (account list upload) with three content themes rotating:
- Theme A: Financial services data observability benchmark insights (drives to gated report)
- Theme B: Customer proof points / case-adjacent content (anonymised or with permission)
- Theme C: Thought leadership from DataLens technical leaders on FS-specific data challenges
Target: 80%+ of target contacts reached at least 2x. CTR benchmark: 0.40% (Sponsored Content). Target 50+ gated report downloads from target accounts.

**5. LinkedIn Thought Leader Ads -- $9,000 ($300/day x 30 days)**
Purpose: Amplify personal brand of DataLens CEO / CTO / Head of FS vertical through their personal posts, targeted to the six account buying committees.
Format: Thought Leader Ads (boosted personal posts) covering financial services data infrastructure challenges, observability best practices, and platform consolidation trends.
Target: 1.0-1.5% CTR (3x company page benchmark). Build personal familiarity so that when the AE references "our CTO's post about X," the buyer has already seen it.

**6. Executive Email Sequences (via AEs, enabled by marketing) -- $3,000**
Purpose: Direct, personalised outreach from AEs to economic buyers and champions, powered by account intelligence from Phase 1.
Format: 4-touch email sequence per contact, staggered across 30 days. Each email references account-specific usage data and a specific expansion value driver. Content marketer writes templates; AEs personalise per contact.
- Touch 1: "Your team's DataLens usage has [specific metric] -- here's what platform-wide looks like"
- Touch 2: Share the account-specific Impact Brief
- Touch 3: Financial services benchmark report with one insight highlighted
- Touch 4: ROI calculator / business case template with a meeting request
Target: 35%+ open rate, 5%+ reply rate. At least 1 meeting booked per account.
Cost: $3,000 for email tooling, data enrichment (Clearbit/ZoomInfo credits for contact verification).

**7. Executive Direct Mail / Gift -- $6,000 ($1,000 per account)**
Purpose: Pattern interrupt for economic buyers. Physical touchpoint cuts through digital noise for high-value deals.
Format: Premium branded package sent to CIO/CTO at each account. Contents: Printed account-specific Impact Brief, a short handwritten note from the AE, and a high-quality branded item (not swag -- something genuinely useful, e.g., a premium notebook or a curated book on data strategy).
Target: 100% delivery. Follow-up call from AE within 48 hours of estimated delivery.

**8. Custom Webinar / Executive Briefing -- $12,000**
Purpose: Mid-funnel acceleration. Create a reason for all six accounts' stakeholders to engage simultaneously in a high-value, exclusive setting.
Format: "Financial Services Data Observability Leadership Roundtable" -- invite-only virtual event for senior data leaders at the six target accounts (plus 2-3 non-competing FS firms for credibility). Feature a DataLens technical leader, an existing enterprise customer speaker (if available), and a third-party analyst or practitioner.
- $5,000: Speaker fees / honorarium for external participant
- $4,000: Event production (platform, rehearsal, recording, post-production)
- $3,000: Promotion and follow-up content (clips, summary report)
Target: At least 3 of 6 target accounts send at least 1 attendee. 15-25% of attendees convert to expansion conversation within 14 days.

### Phase 3: Pipeline Acceleration & Close Support (Days 46-90) -- $60,000

**9. LinkedIn Retargeting -- $12,000 ($267/day x 45 days)**
Purpose: Keep DataLens top-of-mind for all contacts who have engaged with any campaign asset. Shift messaging from awareness to decision-stage.
Format: Retargeting ads served to contacts who downloaded the benchmark report, attended the webinar, or engaged with LinkedIn content. Creative focuses on:
- ROI proof points ("X% reduction in incident response time")

Target: 3x frequency cap per contact per week. Drive contacts back to the ROI calculator or to book an expansion review meeting.

Format: Each AE delivers a tailored expansion proposal to their 1-2 assigned accounts, incorporating:

- Clear pricing: mid-market plan vs. platform-wide, with expansion incentive if applicable
Target: 6 expansion proposals delivered. At least 4 of 6 accounts enter active pipeline (deal stage: "Proposal Delivered" or beyond).

Purpose: Capture any in-market search activity from target account employees. Defensive play to ensure DataLens owns its own brand terms and adjacent competitor comparison terms.
Format: Google Ads on branded terms ("DataLens," "DataLens pricing," "DataLens enterprise") and competitor comparison terms ("DataLens vs. [competitor]," "data observability platform comparison").
Target: Own 100% impression share on branded terms. Capture any evaluation-stage research from target accounts.

**12. Account-Specific Nurture Content -- $18,000**
Purpose: Sustain engagement across the buying committee throughout the 90-day cycle with fresh, relevant content.
Format:
- 2 additional financial services-focused blog posts / articles ($3,000 each, $6,000 total) -- authored by DataLens subject matter experts, promoted via LinkedIn and email
- 6 account-specific email updates (one per account, monthly) showing new usage insights, product updates relevant to their deployment, and expansion case studies ($1,000 each, $6,000 total for content production + design)
- 3 short-form video testimonials or product demos tailored to FS use cases ($2,000 each, $6,000 total)
Target: Every contact at every account receives at least 1 fresh content touchpoint every 2 weeks.

**13. Champion Enablement Kits -- $6,000 ($1,000 per account)**
Purpose: Arm the internal champion at each account with everything they need to sell the expansion internally.
Format: Per-account digital package containing:

Target: Delivered to identified champion at each account by Day 50. Champion confirms they have presented to economic buyer by Day 70.

## SALES-MARKETING INTEGRATION

**Account assignment:**
- 4 AEs, 6 accounts = 2 AEs own 2 accounts each, 2 AEs own 1 account each (assign based on existing relationship strength and account complexity)

**Lead flow: Marketing to Sales:**
1. **Contact enrichment** (Days 1-10): Demand gen manager enriches all 48-72 contacts in HubSpot with verified email, phone, LinkedIn URL, title, reporting line. Use ZoomInfo/Clearbit credits.
2. **Account scoring in HubSpot:** Score at account level (not just contact level). Scoring model:
   - +20 points: Downloaded benchmark report
   - +30 points: Attended webinar / executive briefing
   - +15 points: Clicked LinkedIn ad (any)
   - +10 points: Opened AE email
   - +25 points: Replied to AE email
   - +40 points: Requested expansion review / meeting
   - +50 points: Champion confirmed internal presentation
3. **Routing threshold:** Account score >= 80 = HOT. AE receives Slack notification + HubSpot task to call within 24 hours.
4. **Sequence deployment:** AE email sequences launch on Day 15. Marketing coordinates with AEs on timing so that LinkedIn ads are running before the first email drops (contacts should have seen DataLens content in their feed before the email arrives).
5. **SLA for HOT accounts:**
   - AE responds to any reply within 4 business hours
   - AE schedules expansion review meeting within 5 business days of HOT status
   - AE delivers expansion proposal within 10 business days of first expansion meeting

**Weekly sync (mandatory):**
- 30-minute weekly meeting: demand gen manager + content marketer + all 4 AEs
- Agenda: account-level engagement dashboard review, pipeline stage updates, content requests from AEs, blocker identification
- HubSpot dashboard shared in Slack channel before each meeting

## RISK AND CONTINGENCY

**Primary risk:** Champion turnover or internal reorganisation at one or more target accounts delays the expansion conversation beyond the 90-day window.
Contingency: By Day 14, identify a secondary champion at each account (backup contact who has product familiarity). If primary champion goes dark, AE pivots to secondary within 5 business days. If both are unresponsive, escalate to CS team for a relationship health check.

**Risk 2:** Low engagement with content assets -- benchmark report does not generate downloads or webinar attendance is below target.
Contingency: If benchmark report downloads < 10 by Day 30, shift $5,000 from LinkedIn Sponsored Content to a second, more targeted content asset (e.g., a 1:1 custom analysis for the two highest-priority accounts). If webinar RSVPs < 4 accounts by Day 30, convert to 1:1 executive briefings (30-minute private sessions) instead.

**Risk 3:** Budget approval stalls at the economic buyer level despite champion engagement.
Contingency: Deploy CFO-specific content (ROI calculator, TCO comparison, risk quantification of not expanding). Request CS team share support/incident data that quantifies the cost of the current limited deployment.

**Decision gates:**
- If pipeline created < $1.7M (2 accounts) by Day 45 --> reallocate $15,000 from LinkedIn to 1:1 executive dinners or in-person meetings with top 3 accounts
- If 0 expansion proposals delivered by Day 60 --> emergency review: audit account engagement data, interview AEs, reassess whether accounts are viable expansion candidates within this quarter
- If webinar attendance < 2 accounts by Day 32 --> pivot to 1:1 format (see Risk 2)
