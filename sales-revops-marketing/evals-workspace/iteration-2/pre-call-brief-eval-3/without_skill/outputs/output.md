## RISK FACTORS

### Risk 1: CFO Uses SLA Miss + Adoption Decline as RFP Justification

James Whitfield is a new CFO, 5 months in, auditing all contracts over $500K. He has never met us and has no relationship equity. If he walks into the QBR and sees:
- Adoption dropped 23 percentage points
- A P1 SLA breach at 125% overshoot
- The executive sponsor (Rachel) visibly disengaged

...he has everything he needs to justify an RFP or renegotiation. The data tells a story of declining value, and a cost-focused CFO will read it that way unless we reframe it first.

**Mitigation:** Present the adoption recovery plan and SLA remediation evidence proactively -- before James raises it. Lead with the plan, not the defense.

### Risk 2: Rachel's Silence Signals Internal Champion Loss

Rachel Dunn signed this deal and was the executive sponsor. Three consecutive missed check-ins is not "busy" -- it's disengagement. Possible explanations:
- She's no longer championing the platform internally
- She's frustrated by the P1 incident and has lost confidence
- She's delegated everything to Marcus and considers it operational, not strategic
- She's been approached by a competitor and is evaluating alternatives

If Rachel is no longer our champion, we have no executive air cover when James scrutinizes the contract.

**Mitigation:** Request a 1:1 with Rachel before the QBR. If she declines or is unavailable, use the QBR to directly re-engage her with strategic-level talking points (not operational metrics).

### Risk 3: Competitor Displacement While Adoption Declines

Sentinel Shield and CyberArch are running LinkedIn ads targeting Meridian. Declining adoption creates a window for competitor displacement: users who are not on the platform are users who would not notice a switch. If competitor outreach converts to conversations while our adoption is declining, we could face a competitive RFP with 22 months still on the contract -- plenty of time for Meridian to negotiate an early termination.

**Mitigation:** Demonstrate product value that competitors cannot match. Prepare a side-by-side comparison if asked. More importantly, fix the adoption problem -- competitive ads are only dangerous when the existing vendor is underperforming.

## RECOMMENDED QBR STRATEGY

### Before the QBR

1. **Request a 1:1 with Rachel Dunn** (email today, call if no response within 48 hours). Goal: understand her current sentiment, re-establish executive alignment, and get ahead of any concerns she may raise in the QBR. Frame: "Rachel, ahead of our QBR, I wanted to get 15 minutes with you to make sure we're aligned on priorities for the next phase of the partnership."

2. **Prepare a Value Realization Report for James Whitfield.** This is the document that arms you for the CFO conversation. Structure:
   - **ROI quantification:** Total value delivered since contract start (incidents prevented, time saved, compliance requirements met). Use metrics the CFO cares about: cost avoidance, risk reduction, operational efficiency -- not product features.
   - **Adoption recovery plan:** Acknowledge the 84% to 61% decline with a specific plan to return to 80%+ within 90 days. Include milestones, owner, and success metrics.
   - **SLA remediation:** Document what happened with the November P1 incident (root cause), what changes were made to prevent recurrence, and current SLA performance since November. If SLA has been clean since November, lead with that.
   - **Competitive positioning:** Do not disparage competitors, but quantify switching costs (migration timeline, retraining, integration rebuild). Frame: "We've been embedded in your infrastructure for 14 months -- here's what a switch would cost in time and risk."

### During the QBR

3. **Lead with the adoption recovery plan.** Do not wait for James to raise adoption decline. Open with: "We've noticed adoption has trended down and we've built a specific plan to address it." This reframes the narrative from "vendor is underperforming" to "vendor is proactive about value delivery."

4. **Address the P1 SLA breach directly.** Acknowledge it, explain the root cause, detail the remediation steps taken, and show SLA performance since November. Do not hide from it -- James will find it if you don't surface it.

5. **Close intelligence gaps.** Use the QBR to learn:
   - From James: "What are you looking for as you evaluate vendor partnerships?" (reveals his evaluation criteria)
   - From Rachel: "How are you thinking about the platform's role in your security roadmap for the next 18 months?" (reveals whether she still sees strategic value)
   - From Marcus: "What's driving the adoption change we're seeing?" (reveals root cause)
