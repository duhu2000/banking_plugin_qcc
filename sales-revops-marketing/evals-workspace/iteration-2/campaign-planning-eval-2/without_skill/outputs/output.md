# Thought Leadership Campaign: AI-Powered Revenue Operations for Mid-Market SaaS

## Campaign Overview

**Campaign Name:** "The Revenue Intelligence Gap" -- Thought Leadership Series
**Duration:** 12 weeks (Q-length)
**Budget:** $75,000
**Primary KPI:** 400 net-new MQLs
**Target CPL:** $187.50 or below (benchmark: $180 CPL from prior benchmark report campaign)
**Target Audience:** Revenue operations leaders at mid-market SaaS companies (200-2,000 employees, $20M-$200M ARR)

---

## Strategic Foundation

### Why Research-Led, Not Blog-Led

Our historical data is unambiguous: the benchmark report generated 280 MQLs at $180 CPL -- the best-performing campaign we have ever run. Blog content alone has never moved the needle. This campaign doubles down on the proven formula: original research as the anchor asset, with all derivative content driving back to gated research.

We are not allocating meaningful budget to blog-only content. Every blog post exists to excerpt or promote the research asset, not as a standalone lead gen channel.

### Channel Exclusions

- **Paid search:** Excluded. Brand terms are too expensive in our category.
- **Events:** Excluded. No travel budget available.

---

## Anchor Asset: Original Research Report

**Title:** "The 2026 State of AI-Powered Revenue Operations: Benchmarks from 300+ Mid-Market SaaS Companies"

**Format:** Gated PDF (30-40 pages), with an ungated executive summary (2-3 pages)

**Production Plan:**
- Weeks 1-3: Survey design, distribution (target 300+ responses from RevOps leaders at mid-market SaaS)
- Week 4: Data analysis and report drafting
- Week 5: Design, layout, final review
- Week 6: Publication and launch

**Why this works:** The prior benchmark report proved that original data is the magnet for our ICP. This report updates and expands that formula, covering AI adoption rates, pipeline forecasting accuracy, tool stack consolidation, and RevOps headcount trends -- topics our persona cares about and cannot get elsewhere.

## Channel Strategy & Budget Allocation

| Channel | Budget | Expected MQLs | Target CPL | Role |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Research report (production + promotion) | $20,000 | 150 | $133 | Primary lead magnet -- gated download |
| Content syndication | $22,000 | 120 | $183 | Scale research distribution to net-new audiences |
| Webinars (2 sessions) | $12,000 | 60 | $200 | Mid-funnel conversion from research-engaged leads |
| LinkedIn (organic + sponsored) | $15,000 | 50 | $300 | Top-of-funnel awareness and retargeting to gated asset |
| Email nurture sequences | $6,000 | 20 | $300 | Convert engaged-but-unconverted contacts |
| **Total** | **$75,000** | **400** | **$187.50** | |

### Channel Rationale

- **Research report ($20K):** Heaviest investment mirrors our proven format. Includes survey incentives ($5K), freelance data analyst support ($3K), design/layout ($4K), and promotion ($8K). Expected to be the highest-efficiency channel based on historical $180 CPL.
- **Content syndication ($22K):** Distributes the gated report through platforms like NetLine, TechTarget, or Madison Logic. Targets mid-market SaaS RevOps titles. Historically the most scalable channel for net-new MQLs from research content.
- **Webinars ($12K):** Two webinars -- one at Week 7 (report findings reveal) and one at Week 10 (panel with customers). Includes platform costs, promotion, and speaker prep. Slightly higher CPL but produces higher-intent leads.
- **LinkedIn ($15K):** Sponsored posts promoting the report, carousel excerpts of key findings, and retargeting engaged visitors. Higher CPL but critical for audience reach beyond our existing database.
- **Email nurture ($6K):** Low-cost channel leveraging existing database. Sequences drive to report download and webinar registration. Tooling and template costs only.

## Sales-Marketing Integration

### MQL Definition & Scoring

An MQL is triggered when a contact reaches **score threshold of 75 points** in Salesforce:

| Action | Points |
|---|---|
| Downloaded gated research report | 30 |
| Attended webinar (live) | 25 |
| Watched webinar recording (>50%) | 15 |
| Clicked 3+ LinkedIn posts | 10 |
| Opened 3+ nurture emails | 10 |
| Visited pricing page | 20 |
| ICP firmographic match (200-2,000 employees, SaaS) | 15 |
| Title match (VP RevOps, Director RevOps, CRO, VP Sales Ops) | 10 |

**Threshold:** Contact reaches 75 points = MQL. Routed automatically via Salesforce assignment rules to the six mid-market AEs on round-robin.

### Outreach Sequence (Salesforce + Outreach)

When an MQL is routed to an AE, the following Outreach sequence fires:

1. **Day 0 (within 1 hour):** Personalized email referencing specific content consumed (e.g., "Saw you downloaded our RevOps benchmarks -- the finding on forecasting accuracy gaps tends to resonate with teams your size...")
2. **Day 1:** LinkedIn connection request with brief note
3. **Day 3:** Follow-up email with relevant case study or additional data point from report
4. **Day 5:** Phone call attempt
5. **Day 8:** Final email -- "closing the loop" with calendar link

### Speed-to-Lead SLA

- **MQL to first AE touch:** within 1 business hour
- **Measurement:** Tracked via Outreach sequence initiation timestamp vs. MQL creation timestamp in Salesforce
- **Escalation:** If an MQL is untouched for >2 hours, it reassigns to the next AE in rotation and flags the sales manager

## Risk Management & Contingency

### Decision Gate: Week 4 Review

**Trigger metric:** If net-new MQLs are below 100 by end of Week 4 (25% of 400 target = on-pace threshold).

**Evaluation metrics:**
- MQL count vs. 100 target pace
- CPL by channel vs. $187.50 target
- Syndication lead quality (% meeting ICP firmographic criteria)
- Report download conversion rate on landing page

### Contingency Actions

**If below 100 MQLs at Week 4:**

1. **Reallocate from underperforming channels:** Move up to $5,000 from the lowest-performing channel (measured by CPL) to the highest-performing channel.
2. **If syndication CPL > $220:** Reduce syndication by $5K and shift to LinkedIn retargeting on report downloaders who haven't converted to MQL.
3. **If LinkedIn CPL > $350:** Reduce LinkedIn sponsored spend by $5K and shift to a third webinar (topics derived from most-downloaded report sections).
4. **If report download volume is strong but MQL conversion is low:** Adjust scoring model -- lower threshold from 75 to 60, or increase point values for report downloads.

### Week 8 Mid-Campaign Check

**Trigger:** If below 250 MQLs at Week 8 (62.5% of target).

**Action:** Emergency budget reallocation meeting with demand gen and sales leadership. Options include:
- Accelerating email sends to unconverted report downloaders
- Launching a "report findings" LinkedIn Live event (low cost, high engagement)
- Negotiating bonus leads with syndication vendors based on spend-to-date
