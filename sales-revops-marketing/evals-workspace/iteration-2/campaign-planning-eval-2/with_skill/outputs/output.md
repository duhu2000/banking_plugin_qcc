# AI-POWERED REVENUE OPERATIONS AUTHORITY CAMPAIGN BRIEF

**Goal:** 400 net-new MQLs | **Budget:** $75,000 | **Timeline:** 12 weeks (Q2)

================================================================

## STRATEGY: Thought Leadership-Led

Rationale: Complex B2B SaaS sale to mid-market revenue operations leaders requires trust-first positioning. The historical benchmark report campaign (280 MQLs at $180 CPL) proved that original research is the highest-converting format for this audience -- the strategy doubles down on this proven format rather than experimenting with untested approaches. Blog content alone has not moved the needle historically and will not be a primary channel.

---

## AUDIENCE DEFINITION

**Primary:** VP Revenue Operations / Director RevOps at mid-market SaaS companies (200-2,000 employees, $20M-$200M ARR) currently evaluating or implementing AI in their revenue stack.

**Secondary:** CROs and VP Sales at the same firmographic profile who delegate RevOps tooling decisions but approve budget.

**Estimated addressable audience:** ~12,000-18,000 professionals matching firmographic + title criteria across LinkedIn and third-party syndication databases.

**Target reach:** 800,000-1,200,000 impressions across channels over 12 weeks.

## LEAD MAGNET

**"The State of AI in Revenue Operations 2026: Benchmarks from 500+ RevOps Leaders"**

A gated benchmark report built on original survey data covering: AI adoption rates by company size, top use cases (forecasting, lead scoring, pipeline health), ROI benchmarks by implementation maturity, and a self-assessment framework for readers to score their own RevOps AI readiness.

**Why they would want it:** Mid-market RevOps leaders are being asked by their CROs to evaluate AI tools but have no peer benchmarks to anchor decisions. This report gives them data to build internal business cases and defend tooling investments to their leadership.

**Format:** 25-35 page designed PDF with data visualizations + a 2-page executive summary for C-suite sharing. Estimated production: 2 weeks of writer time (Week 1-2 research and draft, Week 3 design and finalize).

## SALES-MARKETING INTEGRATION

**MQL Scoring Threshold:**
A contact becomes an MQL when they meet ALL of the following:
- Firmographic fit: SaaS company, 200-2,000 employees, $20M-$200M ARR (verified via enrichment)
- Engagement: Downloaded the report OR attended the webinar OR clicked 2+ email nurture touches
- Title fit: Director+ in Revenue Operations, Sales Operations, or Growth Operations

**Salesforce Routing:**
MQLs are routed round-robin to the six mid-market AEs in Salesforce based on territory assignment. If no territory match, route to the AE with the lowest open MQL count.

**Outreach Sequence:**
Each MQL triggers a pre-built Outreach sequence:
- Touch 1 (Day 0): Personalized email referencing the specific report finding most relevant to the prospect's company profile (e.g., "Companies at your stage typically see X -- how does that compare?")
- Touch 2 (Day 3): LinkedIn connection request from the assigned AE
- Touch 3 (Day 5): Follow-up email with a customer proof point relevant to their vertical
- Touch 4 (Day 8): Direct ask for a 20-minute consultation call
- Touch 5 (Day 12): Final breakup email

**Speed-to-Lead SLA:**
- HOT leads (webinar attendees who requested demo or scored 90+): Contacted within 2 hours during business hours.
- Standard MQLs: Contacted within 24 hours via Outreach sequence.
- AE managers receive a daily Salesforce report flagging any MQL older than 24 hours without first touch.

## RISK AND CONTINGENCY

**Primary risk:** Content syndication underperforms. Syndication is allocated $34,000 (45% of budget) based on the assumption it delivers 280-320 MQLs. If syndication partner quality is low or ICP targeting is loose, CPL could exceed $150 and volume could fall short.

**Contingency:** If syndication CPL exceeds $130 by Week 5, pause lowest-performing syndication partner and reallocate that budget to LinkedIn Thought Leader Ads (which have a proven 3x CTR advantage and allow direct ICP targeting).

**Decision gate:** If total MQL count is below 100 by end of Week 4:
- **Evaluate:** Syndication CPL vs. LinkedIn CPL vs. webinar conversion rate.
- **Action:** Reallocate $8,000-$12,000 from the lowest-performing channel to the highest-performing channel. If syndication is the underperformer, shift budget to a second webinar (Week 9) and increased LinkedIn Thought Leader Ad spend. If LinkedIn is the underperformer, increase syndication budget with tighter ICP filters and add a second syndication partner.
- **Escalation:** If below 150 MQLs at Week 6 midpoint, convene marketing-sales alignment meeting to assess whether MQL quality (not just volume) justifies continued spend or whether the campaign goal needs revision.

**Secondary risk:** Research report production delays. If the report is not finalized by end of Week 3, all downstream channels are delayed.
**Contingency:** Writers begin derivative content (LinkedIn posts, webinar outline) in parallel during Week 2-3 using preliminary data, so Weeks 4-5 are not idle even if report design slips by one week.
