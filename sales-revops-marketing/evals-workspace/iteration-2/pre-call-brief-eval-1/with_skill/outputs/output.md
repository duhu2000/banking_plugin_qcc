PRE-CALL BRIEF: Natalie Reeves, VP of Supply Chain | Ironclad Distribution
Call type: Discovery | Duration: 30 min (estimated) | Tomorrow, 2:00 PM ET
================================================================

## QUICK CONTEXT (2-minute read)

Ironclad Distribution is a mid-market third-party logistics provider headquartered in Memphis, TN -- approximately 600 employees, ~$180M in revenue. They run SAP for ERP and Blue Yonder for warehouse management. Their Q3 last year was rough: they missed fill rates on two major retail accounts per their public earnings call, which means demand forecasting failures have already cost them real client relationships. Two months ago they hired a Chief Digital Officer, signaling executive-level commitment to technology modernization. Natalie Reeves is VP of Supply Chain and submitted a demo request through your website after attending your AI-powered demand forecasting webinar. Her form submission specifically said she is "evaluating demand planning tools for Q3 implementation" -- so there is an active evaluation with a stated timeline. She is not cold; she came to you, she has seen your webinar content, and she is likely comparing you against at least one other vendor.

## YOUR GOAL FOR THIS CALL

Qualify the pain behind the missed fill rates, identify who else is involved in the evaluation (especially whether the new CDO is the executive sponsor), confirm the Q3 implementation timeline is real and funded, and book a technical deep-dive or scoped demo as the next step.

## DISCOVERY QUESTIONS -- USE IN THIS ORDER

**Opening (earn the right to ask questions):**

"Natalie, I saw you attended the demand forecasting webinar and mentioned evaluating tools for Q3 -- before I talk about anything on our side, I'd love to understand what's driving the urgency right now. What's happening with demand planning at Ironclad that made this a priority?"

**Depth questions (once top pain is identified):**

1. "How are you handling demand forecasting today -- is that sitting inside SAP, or do you have a separate planning layer on top of it?"
2. "When you missed those fill rates last year, what was the downstream cost -- lost accounts, penalty chargebacks, or something else?"
3. "What have you already looked at or tried to fix the forecasting gap? What didn't work?"
4. "Is this initiative something you're driving from supply chain, or is it part of the broader digital transformation the CDO is leading?"
5. "If you had a reliable demand forecasting system running by Q3, what does success look like for you -- what metrics would you be measuring?"

**Qualification questions (must know before close):**

- "Who else would need to evaluate and sign off on a tool like this -- are we talking procurement, IT, the CDO's office?"
- "Is there budget allocated for this already, or is that part of what you're building the case for?"
- "You mentioned Q3 implementation -- is that a hard deadline tied to a planning cycle, or is there flexibility?"

## ANTICIPATED OBJECTIONS AND RESPONSES

**Objection 1:** "We already have SAP -- can't we just use the demand planning modules we're already paying for?"
-> Response: "That's a fair question -- a lot of 3PLs start there. The challenge most of them run into is that SAP's native forecasting is built for manufacturing planning cycles, not the variability you see in 3PL where every client has different demand patterns. It might be worth exploring what gaps you're seeing in SAP's forecasting specifically, and whether those are the ones that contributed to the fill rate misses."

**Objection 2:** "We're early in the evaluation -- not ready to commit to anything yet."
-> Response: "Completely understand, and I wouldn't expect you to be. The goal today is just to understand what you're dealing with so that if we do a deeper session, it's scoped to your actual environment -- SAP, Blue Yonder, your specific client mix -- rather than a generic walkthrough."

**Objection 3:** "We're also looking at [competitor] -- why should we consider PredictFlow?"
-> Response: "I'd actually love to hear what you're seeing from them -- that helps me understand what matters most to you. Rather than giving you a comparison pitch, let me understand your requirements first and then I can be specific about where we're strong and where we might not be the right fit."

## CALL SUCCESS CRITERIA

**Primary:** Natalie confirms the specific pain (missed fill rates / forecasting gaps), names the buying committee (especially whether the CDO is the sponsor), confirms Q3 timeline is budget-backed, and agrees to a scoped technical demo with the relevant stakeholders in the room.

**Secondary:** Pain is confirmed and timeline is real, but buying committee and budget status are still unclear. Natalie agrees to a follow-up call that includes the CDO or another decision-maker.

**Minimum:** You understand the current forecasting process (SAP-native vs. manual vs. other), confirm that the evaluation is real and not just browsing, and have a defined next step with a date on the calendar.

## COMPETITORS TO LISTEN FOR

- **Blue Yonder Luminate Planning** -- They already use Blue Yonder for WMS, so the "add the planning module from your existing vendor" pitch is the most obvious competitive threat. If mentioned: acknowledge the integration advantage, then probe on whether Blue Yonder's planning module handles the multi-client demand variability a 3PL needs vs. a single-enterprise supply chain.
- **Kinaxis RapidResponse** -- Strong in supply chain planning for mid-market. If mentioned: ask what's drawing them to Kinaxis specifically -- usually it is scenario planning. Understand if their need is more about forecasting accuracy or scenario modeling.
- **o9 Solutions** -- AI-native planning platform, often positioned against legacy tools. If mentioned: explore whether they are looking at o9 for the full planning suite or specifically for demand sensing, since scope differences matter for implementation timeline.
- **Doing nothing / status quo (SAP native)** -- The most likely "competitor." Probe the cost of inaction: what happens if they go through another peak season with the same forecasting approach that missed fill rates last year.

## THINGS NOT TO DO

1. **Do not demo the product on this call.** This is discovery. If she asks for a walkthrough, say "I want to make sure any demo I show you maps to your actual environment -- let me understand your setup first and we'll schedule something tailored."
2. **Do not assume the CDO is the economic buyer.** The CDO hire is a signal, not a confirmed fact about this deal's power structure. Ask, don't assume.
3. **Do not reference the earnings call fill rate miss directly.** You know about it; she may not know you know. Let her bring up the pain. If she doesn't, ask about forecasting challenges generally and let her tell you the story.
4. **Do not trash Blue Yonder or SAP.** She chose those systems. Criticizing them criticizes her judgment. Instead, position PredictFlow as a layer that makes their existing investments work harder.
5. **Do not skip the next-step booking.** Before the call ends, there must be a specific date and time for the next interaction with defined attendees. "I'll send you some info" is not a next step.
