This deal is not healthy, and it is not yet stalled -- but it is moving in the stalled direction. Here is why:

1. **12 days past the original close target with no signature.** A missed close date is one of the strongest predictors of deal slippage. The deal was supposed to close March 28. It is now April 9 with no forward movement on the contract.

2. **Procurement has been silent for 3+ weeks on redlines.** Legal sent back redlines on the data residency clause three weeks ago. You responded within 48 hours. Since then -- nothing. Three weeks of silence from procurement after receiving your response is not normal processing time. Something is blocking progress, and you don't know what it is.

3. **VP Gail Hashimoto went dark after one call.** You multi-threaded to Gail 6 weeks ago. She took one call and then went dark. If Gail is the economic buyer or a key approver, her disengagement is a serious risk. One call followed by silence suggests she was either doing due diligence without intent, or she has concerns she hasn't voiced.

4. **12-day conversation gap with champion.** Derek's last real conversation was 12 days ago, and his message was "we're close, just need procurement to finish." Champion reassurance without procurement movement is a classic stall signal. Derek may genuinely believe the deal is close, but he may not have visibility into what's actually happening on the procurement/legal side.

## RISK FACTORS

### Risk 1: Single-Threaded After VP Went Dark
You attempted multi-threading to Gail Hashimoto 6 weeks ago. She took one call and disappeared. You are now functionally single-threaded through Derek. If Derek does not have the authority or internal influence to push the procurement team, the deal is stuck with no lever to pull. A $340K ARR deal that depends entirely on one champion's internal influence -- without executive-level engagement -- is vulnerable to any internal priority shift.

### Risk 2: "Front-Runner" Language as Potential Stall or Leverage Tactic
Derek told you that you are "the front-runner" and that Aerolytics is also being evaluated. This language should be treated with skepticism. In competitive deals, "you're the front-runner" serves multiple purposes for the buyer: it keeps you engaged and responsive, it can be used to extract concessions ("Aerolytics offered X -- can you match?"), and it does not mean Aerolytics has been eliminated. The fact that procurement has gone silent while you are supposedly the front-runner is contradictory -- if you were truly the chosen vendor, procurement would be working to close, not going quiet.

### Risk 3: Procurement Silence Suggests Internal Blocker
Procurement received your response to their redlines 3 weeks ago. Normal turnaround for redline review at an aerospace company is 5-10 business days. Three weeks of silence points to one of several scenarios: (a) the data residency clause has raised a compliance issue that requires internal escalation, (b) procurement is comparing your terms with Aerolytics terms, (c) a different internal priority has pushed this deal down the queue, or (d) there is an internal disagreement about whether to proceed. All of these are scenarios that Derek, as a Director-level champion, may not have visibility into.
