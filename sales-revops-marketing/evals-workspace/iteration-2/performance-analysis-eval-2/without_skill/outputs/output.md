# LinkedIn Channel Performance Analysis -- Deep Dive

**Spend:** $11,000/month
**Period:** Last 4 weeks
**ICP:** VP/Director of Ops at mid-market logistics companies (200-2,000 employees)
**Question from leadership:** Should we cut LinkedIn?

---

## Executive Summary

**Do not cut LinkedIn.** The channel is generating $378K in pipeline from $11K in spend -- a 34:1 pipeline-to-spend ratio. What you are seeing is not a channel failure. It is a creative fatigue problem inside an otherwise high-performing channel. Cutting LinkedIn before testing creative and audience changes would destroy a pipeline source that is converting at above-average rates downstream.

---

## What Is Actually Going On

### The Decline Is Real -- But It's Creative Fatigue, Not Channel Failure

Week-over-week CTR tells the story:

| Week | Sponsored CTR | Thought Leader CTR | Sponsored Leads | Thought Leader Leads |
|------|--------------|-------------------|-----------------|---------------------|
| 1 | 0.55% | 1.20% | 14 | 8 |
| 2 | 0.46% | 1.00% | 11 | 6 |
| 3 | 0.35% | 0.80% | 7 | 4 |
| 4 | 0.25% | 0.64% | 4 | 2 |
| **Decline** | **-55%** | **-47%** | **-71%** | **-75%** |

Both Sponsored and Thought Leader ads show consistent week-over-week CTR decline. This pattern is textbook **creative fatigue** -- the same audience is seeing the same ads repeatedly, and engagement drops as novelty wears off. Impressions remained relatively stable (38K-41K for Sponsored), which means LinkedIn is still serving the ads to your target audience. The audience isn't shrinking. They're just tired of the creative.

This is a fixable execution problem, not a channel-level failure that warrants budget reallocation.

### CPL Explosion Is a Symptom, Not the Disease

| Week | Sponsored CPL |
|------|--------------|
| 1 | $196 |
| 2 | $250 |
| 3 | $393 |
| 4 | $688 |

CPL rose 3.5x in 4 weeks. This looks alarming in isolation. But CPL is a lagging indicator of CTR decline -- when fewer people click and convert, the same spend produces fewer leads, and CPL inflates mechanically. Fix the creative, and CPL comes back down.

## What Is Working

### 1. Thought Leader Ads Outperform Sponsored at Every Stage

| Metric | Sponsored (Avg) | Thought Leader (Avg) |
|--------|-----------------|---------------------|
| CTR | 0.40% | 0.91% |
| CTR multiple | 1x | **2.3x higher** |
| Cost | $11K/month | $0 (organic boost) |

Thought Leader ads consistently deliver 2x+ the CTR of Sponsored content -- and they cost nothing in direct ad spend. Even while declining, Thought Leader CTR in Week 4 (0.64%) was still higher than Sponsored CTR in Week 1 (0.55%). This format is your highest-performing asset.

### 2. Downstream Lead Quality Is Excellent

| Funnel Stage | Count | Conversion Rate | Benchmark (B2B LinkedIn avg) |
|-------------|-------|----------------|------------------------------|
| Total LinkedIn leads | 56 | -- | -- |
| MQL | 31 | 55% (lead to MQL) | ~40-45% |
| SAL | 18 | 58% (MQL to SAL) | ~35-45% |
| Opp created | 9 | 50% (SAL to Opp) | ~30-40% |

Every downstream conversion rate is **above typical B2B benchmarks**:
- **55% lead-to-MQL** vs. ~40-45% average -- LinkedIn is attracting ICP-fit prospects, not junk leads
- **58% MQL-to-SAL** vs. ~35-45% average -- sales is accepting these leads at a high rate, meaning marketing-sales alignment on quality is strong
- **50% SAL-to-Opp** vs. ~30-40% average -- these leads are turning into real opportunities

This quality signal is critical. A channel that generates low-quality leads at high volume should be cut. A channel that generates high-quality leads at declining volume should be fixed.

### 3. Pipeline-to-Spend Ratio Is Outstanding

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Pipeline generated | $378,000 |
| Monthly spend | $11,000 |
| Pipeline-to-spend ratio | **34:1** |
| Average deal size | $42,000 |

A 34:1 pipeline-to-spend ratio means every dollar spent on LinkedIn generates $34 in pipeline. For context, a 10:1 ratio is generally considered strong in B2B marketing. At 34:1, LinkedIn is your most capital-efficient pipeline source -- and cutting it means cutting $378K in pipeline to save $11K.

---

## What Is NOT Working

### 1. Sponsored Creative Is Exhausted

Sponsored CTR dropped from 0.55% to 0.25% in 4 weeks. The creative has run its course. The same ads, same copy, same imagery being shown to the same audience pool leads to banner blindness.

### 2. Audience May Be Saturated

With impressions holding steady but engagement dropping, the current audience targeting may have reached saturation -- LinkedIn is showing ads to the same pool of VP/Director of Ops at mid-market logistics companies, and everyone in that pool has already seen the ads multiple times.

---

## Recommendation: Save the Channel -- Here's How

### Do NOT cut LinkedIn. Instead, execute a 2-week creative and audience test:

**Week 1-2 Test Plan:**

| Action | Detail | Expected Impact |
|--------|--------|----------------|
| **Refresh Sponsored creative** | Launch 3 new ad variants with different copy angles, imagery, and value propositions. Test: (a) pain-point-led headline, (b) stat-led headline, (c) question-led headline | Recover CTR to 0.40-0.50% range (Week 1-2 levels) |
| **Expand audience targeting** | Add adjacent segments: Supply Chain Directors, Operations VPs at logistics-adjacent companies (3PL, warehousing, fleet management). Stay within 200-2,000 employee range | Increase addressable audience by 30-40%, reduce frequency per user |
| **Shift budget mix toward Thought Leader** | Allocate more promotion behind Thought Leader ad content. If budget allows, boost 2-3 Thought Leader posts per week | Thought Leader already outperforms Sponsored 2.3x on CTR -- amplify what works |
| **Test format mix** | Add carousel or document ads to Sponsored mix -- format novelty resets engagement | Break creative fatigue with structural novelty, not just copy changes |

**Success criteria after 2 weeks:**
- Sponsored CTR recovers to >0.40%
- Combined lead volume recovers to 15+ per week
- CPL returns to <$275 range

**If the test fails after 2 weeks:** Then have the conversation about reallocation. But cut based on test data, not on a creative fatigue cycle that is fixable.

---

## What to Tell Leadership

> "LinkedIn is generating $378K in pipeline from $11K in spend -- a 34:1 return. Lead quality is above benchmark at every funnel stage. What we're seeing is creative fatigue in our ad units, not channel failure. We're executing a 2-week creative refresh and audience expansion test. If CTR and lead volume don't recover after the test, we'll revisit the budget. But cutting a 34:1 pipeline channel before testing creative would be leaving money on the table."
