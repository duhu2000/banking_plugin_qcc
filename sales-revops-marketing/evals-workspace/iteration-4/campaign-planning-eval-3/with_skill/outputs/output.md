AI PIPELINE FORECASTING MODULE -- GTM LAUNCH CAMPAIGN BRIEF
Goal: 60 qualified demos booked (20+ net-new) | Budget: $200,000 | 12 weeks (Weeks 1-12)

STRATEGY: Product-Led Launch with Sustained Demand Generation

This is a new SKU launch requiring a dual-motion strategy: (1) a high-impact launch moment to satisfy the CEO's "splashy launch" requirement, and (2) sustained demand generation through weeks 3-12 to prevent the post-announcement fizzle that killed previous product launches. The strategy explicitly front-loads launch assets but back-loads budget to sustained channels. The brief confirms announcement-only launches fizzled after two weeks -- the entire campaign architecture is designed to counter this pattern.

AUDIENCE DEFINITION

Segment A -- Net-New Logos:
Primary: VP Sales, CRO at B2B SaaS companies, 500-5,000 employees. Decision-makers responsible for pipeline forecasting accuracy and revenue predictability.
Secondary: VP RevOps, Head of Sales Operations -- the evaluators and implementers.
Size: Estimated 3,000-5,000 target companies; 10,000-15,000 target contacts.
Reach: Target 300,000 impressions across paid channels; 1,000+ landing page visits; 20+ qualified demos.

Segment B -- Cross-Sell (Install Base):
Primary: Existing customers on CRM analytics plan -- primary account contact + VP Sales / CRO if different.
Secondary: RevOps/Sales Ops leads at existing accounts who would champion the add-on internally.
Size: 340 existing accounts; estimated 680-1,020 target contacts (2-3 per account).
Reach: 100% of install base contacted via email; 40+ accounts engaged; 40+ qualified demos.

CHANNEL MIX WITH BUDGET ALLOCATION

PRE-LAUNCH PHASE (Weeks 1-3): Asset Production & Teaser -- $35,000

Launch Asset Production ($20,000)
Purpose: Produce all launch-week assets before the launch date with one full week of buffer. Includes product demo video, landing pages (net-new + cross-sell variants), launch announcement content, partner co-marketing materials, email sequences, and ad creative.
Format: Product marketer leads messaging and positioning; content marketers produce copy; designer creates all visual assets; video editor produces 90-second product demo video + 30-second social cut.
Target: All launch assets production-complete by end of Week 2; Week 3 is buffer/QA week.
Rationale: Past product launches that were announcement-only fizzled after two weeks because assets were produced just-in-time with no sustained content pipeline. Front-loading production ensures launch week executes flawlessly AND frees the content team to begin producing sustained-phase content in Week 3. This is not a channel spend -- it is the production investment that enables all channels.

Teaser Campaign -- LinkedIn + Email ($8,000)
Purpose: Build anticipation before launch. Warm the audience so launch day has a primed audience, not a cold start.
Format: LinkedIn: 3 teaser ads (countdown, problem-stat, sneak-peek) targeting ICP. Email: 2-email teaser sequence to install base (Week 2 + Week 3). Blog post: "The problem with pipeline forecasting" (ungated).
Target: 50,000 LinkedIn impressions; 40% email open rate on install base teaser.
Rationale: Teaser campaigns convert launch-day traffic at higher rates because the audience is already aware and curious. This directly combats the announcement-only fizzle pattern -- by Week 4 (launch week), the audience has already seen 2-3 touchpoints.

Partner Co-Marketing Setup ($7,000)
Purpose: Coordinate joint launch activities with Salesforce and HubSpot ecosystem partners. Align on co-branded content, joint webinar, and partner email sends.
Format: Co-branded landing page per partner, joint webinar content development, partner email copy for their lists, integration-specific demo scripts.
Target: Both partners committed to email send to their ecosystem lists during launch week; joint webinar scheduled for Week 6.
Rationale: Partner co-marketing is a pipeline-contributing channel, not a logo exercise. Salesforce and HubSpot ecosystem partners have direct access to our ICP (B2B SaaS companies using those CRMs). Partner-sourced demos will be tracked as a named secondary KPI. Budget covers co-branded asset production and partner coordination.

LAUNCH WEEK (Week 4): Concentrated Impact -- $30,000

Launch Week Media Blitz ($20,000)
Purpose: Maximize awareness and demo bookings in a concentrated 5-day window. All channels fire simultaneously.
Format:
- LinkedIn Sponsored Content: launch announcement + product demo video ($10,000 -- heavy spend for one week)
- Google Ads: branded + category terms ("AI pipeline forecasting," "sales forecasting software") ($6,000)
- Partner email sends: Salesforce + HubSpot ecosystem lists (partner handles send; we fund content production -- included in partner setup budget)
- Own email blast: full install base + ICP segments from database ($0 incremental -- infrastructure already set up)
- Blog + podcast: launch announcement episode + blog post ($0 incremental -- team produces)
- Social organic: CEO and exec team LinkedIn posts coordinated (cross-posted company page) ($0)
Target: 100,000 LinkedIn impressions in one week; 500+ landing page visits; 15+ demo requests in launch week.
Rationale: The CEO wants a splashy launch moment. Concentrating $20K of paid media into a single week creates that spike. However, this is only 10% of total budget -- the architecture ensures the remaining 90% sustains demand through Weeks 5-12, directly addressing the historical fizzle pattern. LinkedIn earns the largest launch-week allocation because it is the most targetable channel for VP Sales/CRO at B2B SaaS companies.

Launch Week Retargeting Setup ($2,000)
Purpose: Pixel all launch-week landing page visitors for retargeting in the sustained phase.
Format: LinkedIn Matched Audiences + Google remarketing audiences configured. No retargeting ads run in launch week (audience is too small); they activate in Week 5.
Target: Build retargeting pool of 500+ visitors by end of launch week.
Rationale: Retargeting launch-week visitors in Weeks 5-12 is the primary mechanism for converting interest that was expressed but not acted upon during the spike. Low setup cost, high downstream value.

Install Base Launch Email Sequence ($8,000)
Purpose: Dedicated cross-sell motion to 340 existing accounts. This is a separate sequence from the net-new launch blast.
Format: 5-email sequence over 10 days, personalized per account's current CRM analytics usage. Email 1: launch announcement + "see what's new." Email 2: ROI calculator (how forecasting module adds value on top of CRM analytics). Email 3: customer case study (early beta user). Email 4: 1-click demo booking for existing customers (streamlined -- no discovery needed). Email 5: limited-time cross-sell pricing offer.
Target: 45% open rate (existing customers); 8% click rate; 25+ demo bookings from install base in Weeks 4-6.
Rationale: The install base is the highest-conversion audience. These are customers who already trust us and use our CRM analytics product. The cross-sell email sequence leverages that relationship with account-specific messaging referencing their current usage. This is the lowest-CPL channel in the campaign and should deliver the majority of the 40 cross-sell demos. The $8K covers sequence production, HubSpot workflow configuration, and A/B testing.

SUSTAINED DEMAND GEN PHASE (Weeks 5-12): Pipeline Machine -- $135,000

Format: LinkedIn Sponsored Content + Thought Leader Ads. Creative rotated every 2 weeks (4 creative cycles). Firmographic targeting: 500-5,000 employees, B2B SaaS, VP Sales/CRO/RevOps titles.

Sustained spend across 8 weeks (not front-loaded into launch) directly counters the announcement-only fizzle pattern. Announcement-only launches fizzled after two weeks -- this sustained allocation ensures LinkedIn remains active for the full quarter.

RISK AND CONTINGENCY

Primary risk: Post-launch demand dropoff -- the exact pattern that killed previous product launches. Demo pace spikes in Week 4 then collapses by Week 6.
Contingency: The campaign architecture already mitigates this by allocating 67.5% of budget ($135K) to sustained demand gen (Weeks 5-12) vs. only 15% ($30K) to launch week. However, if the dropoff is more severe than expected:
Decision gate: If demo pace is below 15 total demos by end of Week 6 (on track for < 30 at quarter-end, far below 60 target), execute the following:
1. Increase retargeting budget by $5,000 (pull from underperforming channel) to re-engage the launch-week audience that did not convert
2. Accelerate Webinar 2 from Week 10 to Week 8 to pull forward mid-funnel conversions
3. Activate an install-base "win-back" email to the ~300 accounts that did not respond to the cross-sell sequence, with a stronger offer (extended trial, dedicated onboarding)
4. Shift $5,000 from LinkedIn Sponsored Content to Google Ads if Google is converting at lower cost-per-demo
5. Request farming AEs to add personal outreach to their top 20 accounts that have not engaged

Secondary risk: Net-new demos underperform (< 10 by Week 6) while cross-sell is on track.
Contingency: Reallocate $10,000 from cross-sell channels (install base email is nearly free; reduce retargeting) to net-new channels (increase Google Ads for in-market capture; expand LinkedIn targeting to adjacent titles). Accelerate partner co-marketing email sends.

Tertiary risk: Partner co-marketing delivers logos but not pipeline.
Contingency: If zero partner-sourced demos by Week 8, deprioritize partner co-branded case study #2 and reallocate that content production time to higher-performing channels. Track partner-sourced pipeline weekly as a named secondary KPI to catch this early.
