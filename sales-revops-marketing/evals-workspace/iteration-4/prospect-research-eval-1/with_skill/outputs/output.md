-- THE SPECIFIC PAIN --

CRITICAL STRATEGIC CONSIDERATION: Datadog is not a straightforward buyer of CI/CD and deployment automation tooling. They are actively building and selling CI/CD Visibility as a product to their own customers. This creates a fundamental competitive tension.

There are two distinct scenarios:

1. **Internal engineering need (BUY scenario):** Datadog's internal engineering teams (~2,000+ engineers) need CI/CD tooling to build, test, and deploy their own platform. At their scale, internal deployment automation is a real operational challenge. The pain here is: managing hundreds of microservices across multiple cloud providers requires sophisticated deployment orchestration that may exceed what their internal tooling team has built.

2. **Competitive overlap (CONFLICT scenario):** Datadog's CI Visibility product monitors and analyzes CI/CD pipelines -- which sits adjacent to CI/CD automation. Selling CI/CD tooling to a company building CI/CD products creates a vendor-who-is-also-a-competitor dynamic. The buying committee may view external CI/CD tooling as something they should build internally to maintain competitive insight.

The sales approach must address this tension directly. The viable angle is: "Even companies that build CI/CD visibility products still use specialized deployment automation for their internal engineering workflows -- building vs. buying internal tooling is a different decision than building a product for customers."
