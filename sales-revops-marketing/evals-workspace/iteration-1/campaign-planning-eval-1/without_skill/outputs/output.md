# DataMesh ABM Campaign Plan: Financial Services Enterprise Accounts

**Campaign Name:** Operation Reconciliation
**Duration:** April 1 -- June 30 (90 days)
**Budget:** $120,000
**Goal:** 3 of 6 named accounts into active pipeline by end of Q2
**Team:** Head of Demand Gen, 1 Content Marketer, 1 Dedicated SDR, 1 Shared Designer (12 hrs/wk)

---

## 1. WHY BROAD LINKEDIN FAILED AND WHAT REPLACES IT

Last quarter's "fintech" targeting was a category-level play against accounts that don't self-identify as fintech buyers. Investment operations teams at these firms search for solutions to *specific operational pain* -- failed trade reconciliations, T+1 settlement pressure, multi-custodian data fragmentation -- not "fintech innovation." The replacement is a 1:6 ABM motion where every dollar and every hour is traceable to one of six named accounts, and every piece of content speaks directly to the data reconciliation problem these teams already know they have.

## 7. ACCOUNT PRIORITIZATION & SEQUENCING

Not all 6 accounts should receive equal intensity simultaneously. Sequence based on:

1. **Tier 1 (start April 1):** Accounts with the highest existing intent signals or where you have any warm connection (even a 2nd-degree LinkedIn link from an AE). Start SDR sequences immediately.
2. **Tier 2 (start April 15):** Accounts with moderate signals. Use the first two weeks of air cover to warm before SDR outbound begins.
3. **Tier 3 (start May 1):** Accounts with the weakest signals. Longest warming period. If still cold by mid-May, consider reallocating some SDR hours to Tier 1/2 accounts.

Recommendation for initial tiering (adjust based on actual intent data in week 1):

- **Tier 1:** JPMorgan AM, Northern Trust (most likely to have acute multi-custodian pain that's budget-authorized)
- **Tier 2:** State Street GA, BlackRock Aladdin (large internal tech teams -- harder to penetrate but massive deal potential)
- **Tier 3:** Vanguard Institutional, Fidelity Workplace (cost-conscious cultures, longer sales cycles, but winnable on ROI case)

## 9. KEY PRINCIPLES (WHAT THIS CAMPAIGN IS AND ISN'T)

**This campaign IS:**
- Account-specific. Every asset, every sequence, every dollar maps to one of six named accounts.
- Pain-led. We lead with the reconciliation problem, not with DataMesh features. These buyers know they have the problem -- we need to prove we understand the nuances of *their version* of it.
- Multi-threaded. No single point of failure per account. We engage 2-3 personas minimum.
- Sequenced for trust. Warming before asking. Value before pitch. Peer proof before demo.

**This campaign IS NOT:**
- Broad demand gen. Zero budget goes to untargeted campaigns.
- Lead-gen optimized. We do not measure MQLs. We measure account-level engagement and pipeline entry.
- A LinkedIn ad campaign with a landing page. Paid media is air cover for a fundamentally human, outbound-driven motion.
- Content marketing for content's sake. Every asset has a named recipient and a specific role in the sequence.
