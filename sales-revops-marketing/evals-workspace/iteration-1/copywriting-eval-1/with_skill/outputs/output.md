# TESTING RECOMMENDATION

## Round 1: Lead Test

**Test A vs. G** (Specificity vs. Question)

- **Variant A** ("Your warehouse ops vs. the top 10%") -- competitive benchmark framing, statement format
- **Variant G** ("Do you know your cost-per-order?") -- self-assessment question, targets a known KPI gap

**Rationale:** This tests two fundamentally different angles -- competitive comparison (external motivation) vs. self-diagnosis (internal motivation). Both are strong frameworks for ops leaders, and the winner tells you which psychological lever works for this audience. This is a pain-vs-gain test and a statement-vs-question test in one round.

**Expected winner:** Variant G. Question-based subject lines tend to outperform statements in cold email to senior ops personas because they trigger self-assessment. But the competitive framing in A is strong for mid-market where peers are visible -- this is genuinely uncertain, which is why it is worth testing.

## Round 2: Follow-up Tests

Once Round 1 declares a winner:

- If **question format wins**: Test G vs. H (same format, different angle -- KPI knowledge vs. peer ranking)
- If **statement format wins**: Test A vs. C (same format, different angle -- benchmark comparison vs. counterintuitive stat)
- Also test the winner against **E** ("VP Ops at a growing 3PL?") -- the ultra-short, hyper-relevant variant. This tests whether specificity of targeting beats specificity of content.

## What to Measure

| Metric | Why |
|---|---|
| **Open rate** (primary) | Subject lines are evaluated on opens. This is the metric that directly reflects subject line performance. |
| **Reply rate** (secondary) | A subject line that drives opens but no replies may be attracting the wrong curiosity. Track replies to ensure the subject line attracts qualified attention. |
| **Unsubscribe/spam rate** (safety) | Monitor for any variant that triggers higher opt-outs -- especially Variant I (urgency) if used. |

## Minimum Sample Size

- **Minimum 1,000 sends per variant** before declaring a winner (2,000 total for a two-variant test)
- At typical cold email open rates (15-25% for well-targeted ops personas), 1,000 sends gives you 150-250 opens per variant -- enough to detect a meaningful difference
- **Do not call a winner before 1,000 sends per variant.** Early results are noise.
- If open rates are below 10%, increase to 2,000 sends per variant before drawing conclusions
- Run each test for a minimum of **5 business days** to account for day-of-week variation in open behavior

## Sending Cadence Note

Split variants evenly across send times and days. Do not send Variant A on Monday morning and Variant G on Friday afternoon -- that tests timing, not copy. Randomize assignment within each send batch.
