## Sequence Design Notes

### Why this structure works for a HOT lead:

1. **Touch 1 opens with her words, not ours.** Referencing her LinkedIn post proves this isn't a spray-and-pray sequence. The Apex case study is the proof point, not the lead.

2. **Touch 2 (LinkedIn) creates a second channel** without repeating the email content. If she saw the email and didn't reply, the connection request gives her a lower-friction way to engage.

3. **Touch 3 introduces a second trigger signal** (the reliability hire) to demonstrate depth of research and reframe the value prop around operational stability, not just scaling. This is a different buying motivation that may resonate more strongly depending on her current priorities.

4. **Touch 4 (phone) is a pattern interrupt.** After two emails and a LinkedIn touch, a human voice stands out. The voicemail is under 30 seconds and the immediate follow-up email with a calendar link reduces friction.

5. **Touch 5 deploys the Georgia Tech connection deliberately late** -- by Day 11 she's already seen substantive value, so the personal connection feels like a genuine aside, not a manufactured rapport tactic. The technical brief gives her a reason to engage even if she's not ready for a demo.

6. **Touch 6 (breakup) uses the 3-option close** to make replying effortless. "Not now" is explicitly validated, which lowers the psychological cost of responding and often converts better than a hard ask.

### Key principles applied:

- **Trigger-first, product-second.** Every touch opens with something about her world, not ours.
- **Escalating intimacy.** Email > LinkedIn > Email > Phone > Email (personal) > Email (breakup). The sequence gets more personal as trust accumulates.
- **No repetition.** Each touch introduces a new angle: scaling wall, reliability/uptime, technical architecture, personal connection, clean exit.
- **The ask never changes.** 20-minute demo, every time. Consistency signals confidence.
- **Respect the no.** The breakup email is genuinely respectful. A HOT lead who doesn't convert now may convert next quarter if the relationship isn't burned.
