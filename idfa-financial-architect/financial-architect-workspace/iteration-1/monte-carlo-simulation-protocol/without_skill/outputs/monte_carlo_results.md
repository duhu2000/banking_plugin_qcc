## Simulation Parameters

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Base Revenue (Year 1) | $10,000,000 |
| COGS % (Year 1) | 60% |
| Revenue Growth Range | 5% - 15% (uniform) |
| COGS Efficiency Improvement Range | 0% - 2% per year (uniform) |
| Number of Iterations | 100 |
| Random Seed | 42 |

## Model Logic

For each iteration, two random draws are made:

1. **Revenue Growth Rate** ~ Uniform(5%, 15%) -- applied each year
2. **COGS Efficiency Gain** ~ Uniform(0%, 2%) -- COGS % decreases by this amount each year

Year-over-year calculations:

```
Year 1:  Revenue = $10,000,000     | COGS% = 60.00%
Year 2:  Revenue = Year1 * (1+g)   | COGS% = Year1_COGS% - efficiency
Year 3:  Revenue = Year2 * (1+g)   | COGS% = Year2_COGS% - efficiency
Gross Profit (Year 3) = Revenue(Y3) * (1 - COGS%(Y3))
```

## Key Insights

1. **Central Tendency**: The mean Year 3 Gross Profit is $5,069,550, with a standard deviation of $324,864 (6.4% coefficient of variation).

2. **Range of Outcomes**: Year 3 Gross Profit spans from $4,507,834 to $5,731,543, a range of $1,223,709.

3. **Downside Risk**: The 5th percentile outcome ($4,612,012) represents a -9.2% deviation from the baseline model.

4. **Upside Potential**: The 95th percentile outcome ($5,618,845) represents a +10.6% improvement over baseline.

5. **Sensitivity**: Revenue growth rate dominates the variance since it compounds over years and scales the entire revenue base, while COGS efficiency improvement has a secondary but meaningful effect on margins.
