### Pillar-Cluster Architecture (Adapted to Constraints)

The standard pillar-cluster model calls for 4-8 cluster pieces per pillar. With a part-time writer handling 1-2 pieces/week, this calendar uses a **reduced cluster of 2-4 derived pieces per pillar** to stay within capacity. This is a deliberate tradeoff: fewer cluster pieces per pillar, but each piece is high quality and directly tied to discovery call generation. Filler content is eliminated entirely.

**Pillar 1 (Weeks 1-4):**
"Regulatory Compliance and Cyber Risk: What Financial Services CISOs Must Prepare for in 2026"
- Format: Long-form blog post (2500w) -- published Week 1
- Target: Both personas (regulatory angle for CISO, operational controls for IT Director)
- Cluster: 3 derived LinkedIn posts + 1 derived blog (Weeks 2-4)

**Pillar 2 (Weeks 5-9):**
"Vendor Risk Scoring for Financial Services: A Framework for Banks, Insurance, and Fintech"
- Format: Long-form blog post (2500w) -- published Week 6
- Target: Both personas (third-party risk for CISO, vendor assessment for IT Director)
- Cluster: 3 derived LinkedIn posts + 1 derived blog (Weeks 7-9)

**Pillar 3 (Weeks 10-13):**
"The Threat Intelligence Gap: Why Financial Services Firms Are Blind to Their Biggest Risks"
- Format: Long-form blog post (2500w) -- published Week 10
- Target: Both personas (threat landscape for CISO, detection/response for IT Director)
- Cluster: 3 derived LinkedIn posts (Weeks 11-13)

### Content Repurposing Strategy

Each pillar asset is the single source of production effort. The part-time writer produces ONE long-form piece, then derives multiple channel outputs from it:

1. **Pillar blog post** (2500 words) -- the core research and writing investment
2. **LinkedIn post #1** -- Key stat or finding pulled from the pillar, reframed as a standalone insight
3. **LinkedIn post #2** -- Contrarian take or "myth vs. reality" angle from a different section of the pillar
4. **LinkedIn post #3** -- Practical checklist or "3 things to do this week" derived from pillar recommendations
5. **Derivative blog** (where capacity allows) -- Deeper dive into one sub-section of the pillar, expanded with additional context

This means the writer does ~70% of the heavy research/writing work once per pillar cycle, then repurposes for the remaining weeks. LinkedIn posts take 30-45 minutes each when derived from existing pillar content. This is the most efficient path to consistent output at part-time capacity.
