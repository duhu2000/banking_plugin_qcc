# DATAMESH FINANCIAL SERVICES ABM CAMPAIGN BRIEF

**Goal:** 3 of 6 named accounts into active pipeline by June 30 | **Budget:** $120,000 | **April 1 -- June 30 (90 days)**

================================================================

## STRATEGY: Account-Based Marketing (ABM) -- Named Account, Multi-Thread

ABM is the correct strategy because the target list is six named enterprise accounts with known, complex pain (data reconciliation across custodians and prime brokers). The prior quarter proved that broad LinkedIn targeting ("fintech") produced irrelevant leads -- these accounts require hyper-targeted, account-specific outreach with multi-contact orchestration across investment operations buyers and their technical counterparts.

The campaign treats each account as a market of one. Content, ads, and outreach are customised to each account's specific reconciliation environment. AEs own two accounts each; the SDR runs coordinated multi-threading across all six.

---

**Secondary persona:** Enterprise Architecture / Head of Technology for Investment Operations. These are the technical evaluators who will assess DataMesh's integration model and real-time capabilities.

**Tertiary influencer:** Front-office portfolio managers and traders who suffer downstream from reconciliation delays (they feel the pain but don't own the budget).

## CHANNEL MIX WITH BUDGET ALLOCATION

### Why the prior LinkedIn approach failed

Broad LinkedIn ads targeting "fintech" reached tens of thousands of irrelevant contacts. With only 6 target accounts, the campaign does not need reach -- it needs depth and frequency against ~35 specific people. Every dollar must touch a person at one of these six companies.

### Channel Architecture

**Channel 1: LinkedIn ABM Ads -- Account-Targeted (Company List Upload)**
**Budget: $18,000 ($6,000/month)**

- Purpose: Surround the buying committee at all 6 accounts with DataMesh awareness before and during SDR outreach. This is air cover, not lead gen.
- Format: LinkedIn Sponsored Content using company list targeting (upload the 6 companies). Rotate 3 ad creatives per month: (1) industry pain-point stat, (2) customer proof point / case study excerpt, (3) thought leadership asset promotion. Use Thought Leader Ads from your personal profile (Head of Demand Gen) where possible -- 3x CTR vs. company page ads.
- Target: 80%+ impression share against the 6 accounts; minimum 15 impressions per contact per month. CTR target: 1.0%+ (Thought Leader Ads), 0.5%+ (Sponsored Content).
- Note: Do NOT use interest-based or industry-based targeting. Upload a matched company list of exactly 6 companies. LinkedIn will match employees. Overlay by job function (Operations, Technology, Data) and seniority (Director+).

**Channel 2: Account-Specific Direct Mail + Gift**
**Budget: $9,000 ($1,500 per account)**

- Purpose: Pattern interrupt. Break through digital noise and create a physical touchpoint that earns a meeting. Timed to arrive the same week the SDR begins outreach.
- Format: Custom-branded package sent to 3-5 key contacts per account. Include a 1-page "Data Reconciliation Complexity Score" custom-built for that account (based on public data: number of custodians, fund count, known technology stack). Include a tactile item (e.g., a high-quality notebook branded "One Source of Truth" or similar). Handwritten note from the AE.
- Target: 50%+ of recipients acknowledge receipt or respond to SDR follow-up within 10 business days.

**Channel 3: Gated Research Asset -- Lead Magnet**
**Budget: $15,000 (research + design + promotion)**

- Purpose: Produce the single anchor content asset for the entire campaign. This is the reason contacts engage. It must deliver genuine intelligence these operations leaders cannot get elsewhere.
- Format: Research report -- "The 2026 State of Data Reconciliation in Asset Management: Benchmarks, Bottlenecks, and the Real-Time Imperative." 12-15 pages. Gated PDF download on a dedicated landing page. Promoted through LinkedIn ads, SDR outreach, and direct mail.
- Production: Content marketer writes. Shared designer (12 hrs/week) designs. Weeks 1-3 production; live by Week 4.
- Target: 15-20 downloads from named-account contacts over the campaign. (This is a small-audience ABM asset -- volume is not the goal; depth of engagement from target contacts is.)

**Channel 4: SDR Multi-Thread Outreach Sequences**
**Budget: $6,000 (tooling, data enrichment, LinkedIn Sales Navigator)**

- Purpose: The primary meeting-generation engine. The SDR runs coordinated, personalised sequences across 3-5 contacts per account simultaneously (multi-threading). Every touch references account-specific intelligence.
- Format: 8-touch sequence per contact over 28 days: Email 1 (pain-point insight) -> LinkedIn connect + note (Day 3) -> Email 2 (research report offer, Day 7) -> LinkedIn engage with their content (Day 10) -> Email 3 (case study / proof point, Day 14) -> Voicemail + email (Day 18) -> Direct mail follow-up reference (Day 22) -> Final email with specific meeting ask (Day 28).
- Target: 12-15 qualified meetings booked across the 6 accounts. Minimum 2 meetings per account attempted; target 3 accounts with meetings converting to active pipeline.

**Channel 5: Executive Webinar / Roundtable (Invite-Only)**
**Budget: $12,000 (production, speaker fees, promotion)**

- Purpose: Mid-funnel acceleration. Create a high-value, peer-driven event that gives target contacts a reason to engage with DataMesh in a low-pressure, high-credibility format. Invite-only positions it as exclusive.
- Format: 45-minute virtual roundtable: "How Leading Asset Managers Are Solving Real-Time Reconciliation." Feature a current DataMesh customer (or industry analyst) alongside a moderated discussion. Cap attendance at 20 (creates exclusivity and ensures named-account contacts get personal attention). Followed by 1:1 debrief offered to each attendee.
- Timing: Week 7-8 (late May). By this point, SDR outreach and LinkedIn ads have built familiarity.
- Target: 8-12 attendees from named accounts. 25% conversion to active opportunity within 30 days.

**Channel 6: Retargeting (LinkedIn + Web)**
**Budget: $5,000**

- Purpose: Re-engage anyone from the 6 accounts who has visited the landing page, downloaded the report, or engaged with LinkedIn content but has not yet taken a meeting.
- Format: LinkedIn retargeting matched audiences + website retargeting pixels. Serve proof-point ads (customer results, ROI stats) and direct meeting-request CTAs.
- Target: 10%+ retargeting CTR (warm audience); contribute to meeting conversion.

**Channel 7: AE Account-Specific Content (Personalised One-Pagers)**
**Budget: $5,000 (designer time + content marketer time)**

- Purpose: Arm each AE with a 1-page account brief and a custom DataMesh value proposition mapped to that specific account's reconciliation environment. Used in meetings and as leave-behinds.
- Format: 6 custom one-pagers (1 per account). Each references the account's public data architecture, known custodian relationships, and specific reconciliation pain. Designer produces; content marketer writes.
- Target: Delivered to AEs by end of Week 3. Used in 100% of first meetings.

**Contingency Reserve: $50,000**

Held for reallocation at Week 6 decision gate (see Risk and Contingency below). If meetings are tracking ahead of plan, deploy into:
- Sponsored analyst briefing or co-branded content with an industry analyst ($20-25K)
- Additional direct mail / gifting wave ($10K)
- Expanded retargeting or LinkedIn InMail to buying committee members who have engaged but not responded ($15-20K)

If meetings are tracking behind plan, deploy into:
- Doubling SDR sequence volume with an additional 2-3 contacts per account
- Executive-to-executive outreach (your CEO or CRO to their C-suite) via personal email
- Event sponsorship at a financial services operations conference in May/June if one is available

## LEAD MAGNET

**Asset:** "The 2026 State of Data Reconciliation in Asset Management: Benchmarks, Bottlenecks, and the Real-Time Imperative"

**Why they would want it:** Operations leaders at these firms live inside the reconciliation problem every day. They manage hundreds of breaks across custodians and prime brokers. A benchmarking report that quantifies the problem -- average break resolution time, cost of manual reconciliation per $B AUM, percentage of operations teams still running batch vs. real-time -- gives them ammunition for internal business cases and a frame of reference against peers. This is intelligence they cannot easily produce internally.

**Format:** 12-15 page designed PDF. Gated behind a minimal form (name, company, email, role). Hosted on a dedicated landing page with an ungated executive summary (first 2 pages) to drive engagement.

**Production effort:** Content marketer: 30-40 hours (research, writing, editing). Designer: 15-20 hours (layout, charts, infographics). Total production: Weeks 1-3. Live by start of Week 4.

Enrich with: current role, tenure, LinkedIn activity, recent conference appearances, published content, known tech stack. Deliver enriched profiles to AEs by March 28.

2. **Engagement scoring:** Track engagement across all channels per contact. Scoring model:
   - LinkedIn ad click: +5
   - Landing page visit: +10
   - Report download: +20
   - SDR email open: +3
   - SDR email reply: +30
   - Webinar registration: +25
   - Webinar attendance: +35
   - Direct mail acknowledgment: +15
   - Any inbound inquiry: +50

3. **Routing thresholds:**
   - Score 0-29: Marketing-owned. SDR continues sequence.
   - Score 30-59: SDR prioritises for phone + LinkedIn outreach. AE notified.
   - Score 60+: HOT lead. AE engages directly within 4 business hours. SDR supports with scheduling.
   - Any reply (positive or neutral): Immediate AE notification. SDR responds within 2 hours; AE takes over conversation within 4 hours.

4. **Weekly account review cadence:** Every Monday, 30-minute standup: SDR + AEs + Head of Demand Gen. Review per-account engagement scores, sequence progress, meetings booked, pipeline status. Adjust channel spend and SDR priorities based on account-level signals.

5. **Meeting-to-pipeline process:** Once a meeting is booked, AE receives the full engagement dossier (every touchpoint the contact has had with DataMesh). AE uses the account-specific one-pager in the meeting. Post-meeting, AE updates CRM with deal stage, next steps, and pipeline value within 24 hours. Pipeline = active opportunity with defined next step and stakeholder map.

## RISK AND CONTINGENCY

**Primary risk:** Insufficient multi-threading. If the SDR only reaches 1 contact per account and that person is unresponsive, the account stalls. Enterprise deals require 3-5 engaged contacts.

**Contingency:** If any account has fewer than 2 engaged contacts by Week 6, escalate to executive-to-executive outreach (CRO or CEO reaches out to a C-suite contact at that account via warm introduction or personal email). Additionally, attempt to engage the account through the webinar invitation as a lateral entry point.

**Decision gate:** If fewer than 6 meetings are booked by Week 6 (May 9), take the following actions:
1. Reallocate $20K of contingency reserve to additional SDR tooling, data enrichment, and expanded contact mapping (find additional contacts at the 6 accounts).
2. Shift LinkedIn budget to InMail for direct meeting requests to engaged-but-unresponsive contacts.
3. Deploy executive-to-executive outreach on the 2 least-engaged accounts.
4. If still below 4 meetings by Week 9, consider adding 1-2 "stretch" accounts from financial services to the target list to increase the probability of hitting 3 pipeline opportunities.

**Secondary risk:** Research report production slips past Week 3, delaying the primary lead magnet.

**Contingency:** If the report is not ready by Week 4, launch SDR sequences using an ungated "Key Finding" one-pager (a single-page extract of the most compelling data point from the report). Continue sequences with the full report offer once it is ready. Do not delay SDR outreach waiting for perfect content.
