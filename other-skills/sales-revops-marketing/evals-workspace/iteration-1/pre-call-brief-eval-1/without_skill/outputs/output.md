## 2. Situational Analysis

### What We Know

- **Manual exception tracking in spreadsheets** is their stated pain. During peak season this becomes unmanageable -- implies they have seasonal volume spikes (typical for 3PL serving retail clients) and their process doesn't scale.
- **Oracle WMS is legacy.** "Good enough for the basics" means it handles putaway, picking, and shipping at a baseline level, but lacks modern execution-layer capabilities: real-time exception management, dynamic slotting, labor optimization, and client-facing SLA dashboards.
- **They lost Redline Sports** over fulfillment accuracy. For a 3PL, losing a named retail client is existential-level pain -- it signals to the market that they can't perform, and it likely triggered internal scrutiny from leadership and/or the board.
- **Natalie is VP of Supply Chain.** She owns the operational outcome. She likely has budget authority or is one approval away (CFO or CEO at a 600-person company). She's senior enough to be a decision-maker or strong champion.
- **Whitepaper download + accepting a BDR call = active buying signal.** She's not just browsing -- she's building a case internally.

### What We Don't Know (Discovery Gaps to Fill)

1. **Decision process:** Who else is involved? IT (Oracle WMS implications), CEO/CFO (budget), Operations Directors?
2. **Timeline:** Is there an internal deadline or trigger event? Next peak season? Board mandate after the Redline loss?
3. **Budget:** Has money been allocated, or is she building a business case?
4. **Scope:** How many warehouses/DCs? What volume (orders/day, SKUs)?
5. **Other clients at risk:** Is Redline Sports an isolated incident or a pattern?
6. **Competitive evaluation:** Are they looking at other vendors? Manhattan Active, Blue Yonder, Körber?
7. **Oracle relationship:** Are they on Oracle WMS Cloud or on-prem legacy? Is there an Oracle upgrade path they've considered and rejected?
8. **Integration landscape:** What other systems (TMS, OMS, ERP, EDI) need to connect?
9. **What "exception" means to them:** Shorts? Mispicks? Damaged goods? Carrier delays? Late shipments? Each implies a different ShipSmart capability.

## 5. Discovery Call Framework (30 Minutes)

### Opening (3 min)
- Thank her for the time. Reference the whitepaper: *"I saw you grabbed our warehouse automation ROI guide a few weeks back -- what caught your eye in there?"*
- Set the agenda: *"I'd love to use our 30 minutes to understand what's going on in your operation and see if there's a fit. If there is, we'll figure out next steps. If not, I'll tell you. Fair?"*

### Situation & Context (7 min)
Goal: Confirm what Marcus learned, fill gaps on scope and environment.

- "Marcus mentioned you're running Oracle WMS today. Can you give me the quick version of your tech stack on the warehouse side?"
- "How many facilities are you operating? What does peak volume look like vs. steady state?"
- "What types of clients are you serving -- mostly retail, e-commerce, or a mix?"

### Problem Discovery (10 min)
Goal: Get her to articulate the pain in her own words, quantify it, and connect it to business outcomes.

**On exception tracking:**
- "Walk me through what happens today when an exception occurs on the floor. Who catches it, how does it get logged, and what happens next?"
- "You mentioned spreadsheets -- how many people are touching those during peak? What falls through the cracks?"
- "What does that cost you? Not just in labor, but in client SLAs, chargebacks, credits?"

**On the Redline Sports loss:**
- "I understand you lost a retail client last quarter over fulfillment accuracy. That's a tough one. Can you walk me through what happened?" (Let her tell the story -- don't rush.)
- "Was that a sudden failure or a slow erosion of performance?"
- "Are there other clients where you're seeing similar risk?"

**On Oracle WMS:**
- "When you say Oracle is 'good enough for the basics' -- where does it stop being good enough?"
- "Have you looked at upgrading Oracle, or is that off the table?"

### Impact & Urgency (5 min)
Goal: Understand the business case pressure and timeline.

- "After the Redline situation, what's the internal conversation been like? Is there a mandate to fix this?"
- "If nothing changes in the next 6-12 months, what does that look like for Ironclad?"
- "Is there a peak season or contract renewal date that's driving urgency?"

### Next Steps (5 min)
Goal: Advance the deal. Don't demo on this call.

- Summarize what you heard back to her (prove you listened).
- "Based on what you've shared, I think there's a strong fit. Here's what I'd recommend as a next step: a 45-minute working session where I show you exactly how ShipSmart handles exception management and accuracy tracking for 3PLs your size. I'd want your [IT lead / ops director / whoever she mentions] in that room too. Can we get that on the calendar for next week?"
- If she pushes for a demo now: "I could show you the product right now, but honestly it would be a generic tour. Give me a week and I'll build it around your Oracle environment and your specific exception types. That's going to be 10x more valuable for you."

## 7. Competitive Landscape (3PL Mid-Market)

Be aware she may be evaluating:

- **Manhattan Active Warehouse Management** -- strong enterprise play, but heavy and expensive for mid-market. Long implementation timelines.
- **Blue Yonder (formerly JDA)** -- broad supply chain suite. Often oversold to mid-market. Complex.
- **Korber (formerly HighJump)** -- strong 3PL heritage. Legitimate competitor. Ask about them specifically.
- **Deposco** -- cloud-native, mid-market focused. Growing in 3PL. Could be in the mix.
- **Oracle WMS Cloud upgrade** -- the "do nothing new" path. If Oracle is pushing them to upgrade, that's your biggest competitor.

**Your differentiator:** ShipSmart is a warehouse *execution* platform, not a full WMS replacement. The overlay positioning is critical -- she doesn't want a rip-and-replace project. She wants her Oracle "basics" to keep working and to add the intelligence layer on top.
