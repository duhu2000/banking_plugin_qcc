# Q1 ABM Campaign Performance Analysis — Week 4 of 8

## Executive Summary

The campaign is **underperforming on MQL volume** but **outperforming on budget efficiency and pipeline quality**. At the midpoint, 18 of 40 target MQLs are in (45% of goal at 50% of timeline), with only 44% of budget consumed. The MQL-to-SAL conversion rate of 38.9% is below the 45% target but early-stage pipeline value ($340K from 3 opportunities) signals strong lead quality. The core issue is not lead quality — it is top-of-funnel volume and mid-funnel conversion on the landing page.

## 5. Recommended Actions for Weeks 5-8

### Priority 1: Fix the Landing Page (Impact: High, Cost: Low)

The landing page at 12.9% vs. 20% target is the single biggest drag on the entire funnel. Every channel feeds into it.

**Actions:**
- Reduce form fields to 3-4 maximum (name, email, company, title). Every additional field drops conversion ~5-10%.
- A/B test the headline — ensure it matches the exact language used in the LinkedIn ads driving traffic.
- Add social proof above the fold (client logos, "downloaded by X leaders" counter).
- Check page load speed; aim for under 2 seconds.
- Consider a partial ungating strategy: show the executive summary, gate the full report.
- **Target: Get to 18-20% conversion by Week 6.**

### Priority 2: Reallocate Budget from Sponsored Content to Thought Leader Ads

The Thought Leader Ads produce MQLs at $475 vs. $1,600 for Sponsored Content — a 3.4x efficiency advantage.

**Recommended reallocation of remaining $17,800:**

| Channel | Current 4-Wk Spend | Proposed Next 4-Wk Spend | Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| LinkedIn Sponsored Content | $4,800 | $2,500 | -48% |
| LinkedIn Thought Leader Ads | $1,900 | $5,500 | +189% |
| Email (tooling/list expansion) | ~$0 | $500 | New |
| Webinar #2 (Week 6) | $0 | $2,500 | New |
| Landing Page Optimization | $0 | $800 | New |
| Retargeting (webinar no-shows + page bouncers) | $0 | $2,000 | New |
| Reserve | — | $4,000 | Buffer |
| **Total** | **$6,700** | **$17,800** | — |

**Projected MQLs from reallocation:**
- Thought Leader Ads at $5,500 spend / $475 CPL = ~11-12 MQLs
- Email expansion (new list segments + sequence fix) = ~4-5 MQLs
- Webinar #2 (smaller, more targeted) = ~3-4 MQLs
- Retargeting campaigns = ~2-3 MQLs
- Sponsored Content (reduced) = ~1-2 MQLs
- **Projected total Wk 5-8: 21-26 MQLs**
- **Projected campaign total: 39-44 MQLs** (within striking distance to above target)

### Priority 3: Fix Email Sequence Decay

**Actions:**
- Rewrite Email 3 entirely. The 56% CTR drop from Email 2 to Email 3 suggests the CTA or content is misaligned.
- Test a different Email 3 approach: instead of another content push, try a direct "book a 15-min call" CTA or a case study with concrete ROI numbers.
- Add an Email 4 for non-converters: short, personal-tone email from an SDR (not marketing), referencing the specific content they downloaded.
- Segment the list: separate contacts who clicked Email 1 or 2 but didn't convert — they need a different message than cold contacts.

### Priority 4: Run a Second Webinar (Week 6)

**Rationale:** The first webinar produced 5 MQLs at a strong conversion rate and 11 follow-up calls. A second, smaller webinar targeting a narrower segment could replicate this.

**Actions:**
- Topic: Go deeper and more specific than the first webinar (e.g., a live benchmark walkthrough for their industry vertical).
- Invite the 37 webinar attendees who didn't convert + warm email list contacts.
- Use Thought Leader Ads (CEO) to promote — this pairing should compound engagement.
- Target: 50+ registrations, 30+ attendees, 3-5 MQLs.

### Priority 5: Activate Retargeting

**Actions:**
- Build retargeting audiences from: (1) 37 webinar attendees who didn't book calls, (2) 418 landing page visitors who bounced, (3) LinkedIn clickers who didn't download.
- Run small-budget retargeting ads ($2,000) with case study content or the benchmark report.
- These are warm audiences — expected CPL should be significantly lower than cold traffic.

### Priority 6: Improve MQL-to-SAL Conversion (38.9% vs. 45% target)

**Actions:**
- Review the 11 MQLs that did not convert to SAL. Identify common disqualification reasons — is it firmographic fit, timing, or SDR follow-up speed?
- Implement a 24-hour SLA for SDR follow-up on new MQLs. Response time is the #1 predictor of MQL-to-SAL conversion.
- Provide SDRs with the specific content each MQL downloaded so they can personalize outreach.
