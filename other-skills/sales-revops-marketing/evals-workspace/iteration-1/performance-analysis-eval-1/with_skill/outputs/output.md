TOP 5 OPTIMISATION OPPORTUNITIES

1. LINKEDIN SPONSORED CONTENT -- CPL IS 4x TARGET

Analysis: LinkedIn Sponsored is consuming 34% of spend ($4,800) but producing only 17% of MQLs (3). The CPL of $1,600 is 4x the campaign target. CTR of 0.35% is squarely average for the format -- impressions are not the problem. The funnel breaks between click and MQL: 158 clicks yielded only 12 downloads (7.6% click-to-download) and 3 MQLs (25% download-to-MQL). The click-to-download rate suggests the post-click experience (landing page or content offer) is not aligned with what the ad promised.

Diagnosis: The ad creative is generating average-quality clicks, but the post-click content offer does not deliver on the expectation set by the ad. This is a message-to-landing-page alignment problem, not an audience or bidding problem.

Change: Audit the top 3 Sponsored Content ads by click volume. For each, compare the specific promise in the ad copy to the headline and first paragraph of the landing page. Rewrite the landing page hero section to mirror the exact language of the ad. If the ad says "benchmark data for [industry]", the landing page headline must say "benchmark data for [industry]" -- not a generic "download our report". Implement by end of Week 5.

Owner: Demand Gen / Content team
Deadline: Week 5 (creative live by Monday of Week 6)
Expected: Click-to-download improvement from 7.6% to 12-15%, yielding ~5-7 additional MQLs over remaining 4 weeks at current spend rate. CPL reduction to $700-900 range.

2. LANDING PAGE CONVERSION -- 7.1pp BELOW TARGET

Analysis: 480 visits, 62 downloads (12.9% conversion), 61% bounce rate. The target is 20% conversion. Bounce rate of 61% means 293 visitors left without scrolling or clicking. Of those who stayed (187), 62 downloaded -- a 33% conversion rate among engaged visitors. The problem is not the offer itself (33% conversion among engaged visitors is strong); the problem is that 61% of visitors bounce before engaging.

Diagnosis: High bounce + decent engaged-conversion = the page is not loading the value proposition fast enough. Visitors are making a stay/leave decision in the first 2-3 seconds and the above-the-fold content is not compelling enough to hold them. Common causes: generic headline, no social proof above the fold, form visible before value is established, slow load time on mobile.

Change: Restructure the landing page above-the-fold section: (a) Replace the current headline with a specific, outcome-oriented statement -- e.g., "How 47 B2B teams reduced CAC by 31% -- the benchmark data" instead of "Download our benchmark report". (b) Add one proof point above the fold -- e.g., "Based on data from 200+ B2B companies" or a recognisable logo bar. (c) Move the form below the first value section or use a sticky CTA button instead of an inline form. (d) Run a PageSpeed test; if mobile load exceeds 3 seconds, compress hero image and defer non-critical scripts.

Owner: Growth / Web team
Deadline: Changes live by end of Week 5
Expected: Bounce rate reduction from 61% to 45-50%, overall conversion improvement from 12.9% to 18-22%, yielding ~25-40 additional downloads over 4 weeks (at current traffic), producing an estimated 4-6 additional MQLs.

3. EMAIL NURTURE SEQUENCE -- EMAIL 3 CTR COLLAPSE

Analysis: Email open rate is 34% (average for B2B -- benchmark 28-35%). Email 1 CTR is 6.2% (good -- benchmark 6%), Email 2 is 4.1% (average), Email 3 drops to 1.8% (poor -- benchmark floor is 2%). The open rate remains stable (people are opening), but click-through collapses by Email 3. This means the content in Email 3 is not compelling enough to drive action, not that the audience has disengaged (they are still opening).

Diagnosis: Email 3 likely suffers from "nurture fatigue" -- by the third touch, generic educational content no longer provides enough new value to drive a click. The audience has already consumed the general positioning; they need a reason to act now, not another reason to learn.

Change: Rewrite Email 3 to shift from educational content to a specific, anonymised case study using this structure: "[Company type in their industry] was facing [specific problem the ICP recognises]. After [specific action], they achieved [quantified outcome] in [timeframe]." End with a single CTA: "See how this applies to [recipient's company type] -- book 15 minutes." Remove all secondary links. One story, one CTA.

Owner: Email Marketing / Content
Deadline: New Email 3 live by Week 5 send
Expected: Email 3 CTR improvement from 1.8% to 3.5-4.5%, yielding an estimated 2-3 additional MQLs over the remaining sends.

4. MQL-TO-SAL CONVERSION -- BELOW 45% TARGET AT 38.9%

Analysis: 18 MQLs, 7 converted to SAL (38.9%). Target is 45%. The benchmark range for MQL-to-SAL is 35-50% (average). We are in the lower half of average and below our own target. With only 18 MQLs, this is a small sample -- but the gap is meaningful because it compounds: every 5pp improvement in MQL-to-SAL at our current volume adds ~1 SAL per 20 MQLs.

Diagnosis: Two possible root causes to investigate: (a) Lead scoring threshold is too low -- MQLs are being passed to sales before they are genuinely sales-ready, resulting in SAL rejection. (b) Sales follow-up speed -- if SDRs are not responding to MQLs within 4 hours, conversion rates drop significantly. Check CRM for average time-to-first-touch on the 11 MQLs that did not convert to SAL.

Change: (a) Pull the 11 non-converted MQLs and audit their lead scores and engagement history. If 6+ had fewer than 3 content touches before MQL, raise the scoring threshold by adding a "minimum 3 engagements" rule. (b) Check time-to-first-touch in CRM -- if average exceeds 4 hours, implement a same-day SLA with SDR team and route MQL alerts to Slack/Teams with a 2-hour response target.

Owner: RevOps (scoring audit) / SDR Manager (follow-up speed)
Deadline: Scoring audit by end of Week 5; SLA implementation by Week 5
Expected: MQL-to-SAL improvement from 38.9% to 45-50% over remaining weeks, yielding 1-2 additional SALs from the same MQL volume.

5. WEBINAR FOLLOW-UP PIPELINE -- UNREALISED VALUE

Analysis: 52 attendees, 11 booked follow-up calls (21% call booking rate), 5 MQLs. That leaves 41 attendees who did not book a call and 36 who attended but did not convert to MQL. These are warm contacts who invested 30-60 minutes -- they have demonstrated intent. The 58% show rate is average (benchmark 55-65% for B2B), so the topic resonated.

Diagnosis: Post-webinar follow-up likely used a single generic email blast ("thanks for attending, here's the recording"). Attendees who didn't book immediately need a differentiated follow-up path based on engagement signals (poll responses, Q&A participation, time spent on webinar).

Change: Segment the 41 non-booking attendees into two groups: (a) High engagement (asked questions, responded to polls, stayed >75% of session) -- send a personal 1:1 email from the presenter referencing their specific question or poll response, offering a tailored follow-up call. (b) Low engagement (joined late, left early, no interaction) -- add to the standard email nurture sequence starting at Email 2. Implement within 5 business days to avoid signal decay.

Owner: Events / SDR team
Deadline: Segmentation and first emails sent by end of Week 5
Expected: 4-6 additional call bookings from high-engagement segment, yielding 2-3 additional MQLs.
