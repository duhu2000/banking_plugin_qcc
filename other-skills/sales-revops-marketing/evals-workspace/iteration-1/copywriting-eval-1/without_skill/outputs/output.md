## A/B Testing Recommendation

### Phase 1: Test Framework Type (Week 1)

Run a three-way test across the framework categories to determine which angle resonates with this persona.

**Test cell A:** #1 "Your warehouse has a hidden bottleneck" (curiosity)
**Test cell B:** #3 "23-point warehouse ops benchmark" (specificity)
**Test cell C:** #6 "How does your warehouse stack up?" (question)

**Why these three:** Each is the cleanest representative of its framework. No confounding variables like brand name or overly clever phrasing. This isolates the framework effect.

### Phase 2: Optimize Within Winning Framework (Week 2-3)

Take the winning framework from Phase 1 and test the remaining variants in that category against each other. If curiosity wins, test #1 vs #2 vs #10. If specificity wins, test #3 vs #4 vs #5 vs #9. And so on.

### What to Measure

| Metric | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| **Open rate** | Primary metric -- subject line's sole job is to get the open |
| **Reply rate** | Secondary -- confirms the subject line attracted the right intent, not just clicks |
| **Unsubscribe rate** | Guardrail -- a high open rate with high unsubs means the subject line is misleading |
| **Open-to-reply ratio** | Diagnostic -- reveals subject/body alignment issues |

Do NOT optimize for click-through on links in Phase 1. That conflates subject line performance with body copy performance. Isolate the variables.

### Minimum Sample Size

For cold email to VP-level logistics personas, expect baseline open rates in the 15-25% range.

**To detect a 5 percentage point lift (e.g., 20% vs 25%) at 95% confidence and 80% power:**

- **Minimum per variant: ~725 sends**
- **Three-way test (Phase 1): ~2,175 total sends**

If your list is smaller than that, you have two options:

1. **Reduce to a two-way test** (drop one framework, test the other two) -- minimum ~1,450 total sends.
2. **Accept 90% confidence instead of 95%** -- drops requirement to ~550 per variant (~1,650 total).

If your total addressable list is under 1,500, run a two-variant test with 90% confidence and accept directional results rather than statistical significance. At this persona's seniority and niche, that may be the practical reality.

### Send Timing

For VP-level logistics decision-makers:
- **Tuesday or Wednesday**, 7:00-8:30 AM recipient local time
- Warehouse ops leaders start early. Catch them during planning time, not firefighting time.
- Hold send timing constant across all variants. Do not confound timing with subject line.

## Implementation Notes

- Randomize variant assignment across segments, company sizes, and geographies. Do not let one variant get all the larger companies.
- Use the same sender name, preview text, and body copy across all variants in a given phase. Only the subject line should change.
- Wait a minimum of 48 hours before pulling open rate data. Some enterprise mail systems delay delivery. 72 hours is safer.
- Tag each variant in your CRM/sequencing tool for downstream conversion tracking even if you are only optimizing for opens now.
