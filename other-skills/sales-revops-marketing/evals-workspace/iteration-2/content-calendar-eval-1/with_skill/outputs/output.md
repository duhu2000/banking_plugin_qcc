**Total sustainable weekly output:** 5-6 original pieces/week (blog posts, newsletter editions, LinkedIn original posts) plus 3x/week LinkedIn company page posts (mix of original and repurposed).

**YouTube reality check:** Channel is brand new. Producing quality video weekly is not sustainable with this team. Plan for 2 videos total across 8 weeks -- one pre-launch teaser and one launch demo. **CAPACITY FLAG:** This plan is at the upper bound of sustainable output. If the writer is pulled into other projects or if review cycles with CEO/Head of Product slip, cut LinkedIn company page frequency to 2x/week first, then reduce blog to 1x/week. Pillar content and email newsletter are non-negotiable -- they drive the most pipeline value.

**Format:** Gated guide/report (PDF), ~3,000 words
**Angle:** Why mid-market legal ops teams are underserved by enterprise CLM tools and overwhelmed by manual processes. Data-backed. Positions ClauseIQ as the AI-native alternative purpose-built for their scale.
**Owner:** Content writer (research + draft), CEO (foreword), Head of Product (technical validation)

### Pillar 2 (Weeks 5-8): "AI-Native vs. AI-Added: Why Architecture Matters in Contract Analysis"
**Format:** Ungated long-form blog post + companion video demo
**Angle:** Technical differentiation piece. Explains why AI bolted onto legacy CLM (Ironclad's approach) produces inferior analysis vs. AI-native architecture (ClauseIQ). Specific examples of contract clause analysis accuracy, speed, and contextual understanding.
**Owner:** Content writer (draft), Head of Product (technical substance + demo), CEO (distribution)
**Cluster content derived:** 6 pieces (2 blog posts, 2 LinkedIn posts, 1 newsletter feature, 1 YouTube video)

## Competitive Positioning Notes

The calendar deliberately avoids naming Ironclad directly in any published content. The differentiation strategy is:

- **Weeks 1-4:** Establish the mid-market problem (Ironclad serves enterprise; we serve mid-market -- implied, not stated)
- **Weeks 5-8:** Introduce the "AI-native vs. AI-added" framework (Ironclad acquired/bolted AI; ClauseIQ was built AI-first -- implied through architecture discussion, not competitor naming)
- **Blog SEO play:** The "AI-native contract analysis" and "mid-market contract management" blog posts are designed to capture search traffic from prospects researching alternatives to enterprise tools

This approach builds authority without triggering competitive response or looking petty. Sales teams can use the pillar content and comparison framework in direct conversations where naming competitors is appropriate.

## Recommendations

1. **Start building the email list now.** 4,200 subscribers is a solid base, but add a newsletter signup CTA to every blog post and LinkedIn bio. Target 5,500+ by launch day.
2. **Do not add channels.** The temptation to add Twitter/X, a podcast, or paid ads will arise. Resist until post-launch. This team is at capacity. An abandoned channel signals weakness.
3. **Repurpose aggressively.** Every blog post should yield 2-3 LinkedIn posts. Every pillar asset should yield 6+ derivative pieces. The social media person's primary job is repurposing, not original creation.
4. **Gate Pillar 1, ungate Pillar 2.** Pillar 1 (the guide) generates email leads. Pillar 2 (the AI-native comparison) generates SEO traffic and social sharing. Different assets serve different funnel stages.
5. **Post-launch:** Plan a Week 9-12 follow-up calendar focused on customer stories, onboarding content, and "first 30 days" use cases. Begin planning this in Week 6 so it's ready when launch attention peaks.
