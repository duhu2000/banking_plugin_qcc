# Ideal Customer Profile & Primary Buyer Persona

## AI-Powered Contract Review Tool

---

# PART 1: IDEAL CUSTOMER PROFILE (ICP)

## Company Firmographics

| Attribute | Ideal Range | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Company Size** | 200 - 1,000 employees | Mid-market sweet spot. Large enough to have real contract volume and budget, small enough that procurement cycles don't drag 6-12 months. |
| **Annual Revenue** | $20M - $500M | Correlates with the employee range; companies at this stage have dedicated legal spend but aren't locked into multi-year enterprise CLM deals. |
| **Industry Verticals** | SaaS, Fintech, Professional Services (consulting, accounting, staffing) | These verticals are contract-heavy by nature: SaaS has high-velocity vendor and customer agreements, fintech has regulatory-driven contract complexity, and professional services run on SOWs and MSAs. |
| **Geography** | US and UK initially (English-language contracts) | Expand to EU/APAC only after product supports multi-language clauses and jurisdiction-specific risk flags. |

## Operational Qualifiers

| Qualifier | Threshold | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| **In-House Legal Team Size** | 3+ attorneys or legal professionals | Below 3, there's no one with bandwidth to champion a new tool. At 3+, the team feels the pain of manual review acutely and has enough internal credibility to push a purchase through. |
| **Monthly Contract Volume** | 500+ contracts/month | This is the inflection point where manual review becomes unsustainable. Below 500, the pain isn't acute enough to justify new tooling spend. |
| **Existing CLM Platform** | None, or lightweight/homegrown only | Companies already running Ironclad, Icertis, Agiloft, or similar enterprise CLM are disqualified. They want rip-and-replace projects that stall in evaluation for 9-18 months. |
| **Contract Types** | NDAs, MSAs, SOWs, vendor agreements, renewals, amendments | Our tool needs to map to high-volume, repeatable contract types, not one-off M&A docs or bespoke regulatory filings. |

## Technology Environment

| Signal | Ideal State |
|---|---|
| **E-Signature Tool** | Already using DocuSign, Adobe Sign, or similar (they've solved signing; they need review/risk) |
| **Document Storage** | Google Workspace, SharePoint, or Dropbox (we need integration points) |
| **CRM/ERP** | Salesforce, HubSpot, or NetSuite (indicates operational maturity) |
| **No Enterprise CLM** | Critical. If they have Icertis/Ironclad/Agiloft, disqualify or deprioritize. |

## Trigger Events (When to Engage)

These are the moments that transform a latent need into an active buying process:

1. **Compliance scare or audit finding** - A regulatory audit surfaced contract gaps, missed obligations, or non-standard terms that created liability exposure. This is the #1 trigger based on closed-won data.
2. **Costly missed renewal** - An auto-renewal on unfavorable terms, or a missed opt-out window, that resulted in a quantifiable financial hit ($50K+ typically gets executive attention).
3. **Legal team headcount freeze** - Leadership says "do more with the same team" after denying a headcount request. The team is now actively looking for force-multiplier tools.
4. **New GC or VP of Procurement hired** - New leadership in legal or procurement almost always initiates a tech stack review in their first 90 days.
5. **Rapid growth / M&A activity** - Contract volume is spiking due to growth, new product lines, or acquisitions, and the existing manual process is breaking.
6. **Vendor consolidation initiative** - Finance or ops is pushing to rationalize vendor contracts, requiring a full contract audit the legal team can't do manually.

## Disqualification Criteria (Anti-ICP)

Hard disqualifiers that should remove a lead from the pipeline immediately:

| Disqualifier | Reason |
|---|---|
| **Enterprise with existing CLM platform** | Rip-and-replace cycles are 12-18 months. CAC is too high and win rates are low. These deals tie up AE capacity for minimal return. |
| **Solo legal counsel at startups (<50 employees)** | No budget, no internal champion with purchasing authority, and contract volume doesn't justify the tool. |
| **E-signature-only need** | They're looking for DocuSign/PandaDoc, not contract intelligence. Product-market mismatch. Educating them is not worth the sales cycle. |
| **Government / heavily regulated procurement** | RFP-driven buying cycles of 6-12+ months with low margins. Not viable at current stage. |
| **Companies processing <200 contracts/month** | Pain isn't acute enough. They'll evaluate but won't close because the status quo (manual review) still feels manageable. |

## ICP Scoring Model

Use this to score inbound leads and prioritize outbound targeting:

| Criterion | Weight | Score 5 (Ideal) | Score 3 (Acceptable) | Score 1 (Poor Fit) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Company size | 20% | 200-1,000 employees | 100-200 or 1,000-2,000 | <100 or >2,000 |
| Industry | 20% | SaaS, Fintech, Prof Services | Healthcare, Manufacturing | Government, Education |
| Legal team size | 15% | 5+ people | 3-4 people | 1-2 people |
| Contract volume | 15% | 500+/month | 200-500/month | <200/month |
| No existing CLM | 15% | No CLM at all | Lightweight/homegrown | Enterprise CLM in place |
| Trigger event present | 15% | Active trigger (compliance scare, missed renewal) | Exploring improvements | No urgency |

**Scoring thresholds:**
- **4.0 - 5.0:** Tier 1 - Fast-track to AE, prioritize outbound
- **3.0 - 3.9:** Tier 2 - Qualify further, nurture if not ready
- **Below 3.0:** Tier 3 - Deprioritize or disqualify

# PART 2: PRIMARY BUYER PERSONA

## "Diana the Deal-Drowner"

### Persona Snapshot

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Job Title** | Head of Legal, VP of Legal, Senior Legal Counsel, or VP of Procurement |
| **Reports To** | General Counsel, CFO, or COO |
| **Team Size** | Manages 3-8 legal professionals or procurement specialists |
| **Age Range** | 35-50 |
| **Education** | JD from a mid-tier to top-tier law school; many did 3-7 years at a firm before going in-house |
| **Tenure in Role** | 2-5 years at current company; long enough to feel the pain, senior enough to have budget influence |
| **Compensation** | $180K - $300K total comp |

### Professional Context

**Day-to-day reality:**
Diana manages contract review for a company doing 500-1,500 contracts per month. Her team is perpetually underwater. Every Monday, the queue has grown over the weekend. Business teams (sales, partnerships, procurement) are constantly pressuring legal to "just approve it" faster. She spends 60% of her time on low-value, repetitive contract review (NDAs, standard vendor agreements, renewals) and 40% on the strategic legal work she was actually hired to do.

**What keeps her up at night:**
- A non-standard liability clause slipping through because her team was rushing
- Missing a renewal opt-out window on a six-figure vendor contract (this may have already happened once)
- A compliance audit finding contract terms that violate company policy
- Her best attorney quitting because the work is tedious and the volume is crushing
- The CEO asking "why does legal take so long?" in an all-hands

**Her internal reputation:**
Diana is respected but seen as a bottleneck. Sales leadership has complained that legal slows down deal velocity. She knows this perception exists and it frustrates her because she's protecting the company from risk, but she lacks the data to quantify what her team catches and prevents.

### Buying Behavior

**How she finds solutions:**
- Peer referrals from other Heads of Legal (legal community is tight-knit: ACC, CLOC, local GC roundtables)
- Content from legal operations thought leaders on LinkedIn
- Google searches after a specific painful incident ("contract review automation," "AI contract analysis," "reduce contract review backlog")
- Occasionally through vendor outreach, but only if the timing aligns with an active pain point

**Her evaluation process:**
1. Does it actually work on our contract types? (She'll test it on real contracts during a trial)
2. Can my team adopt it without 6 months of change management?
3. Does it integrate with our existing stack (DocuSign, Google Drive/SharePoint)?
4. What's the risk? (She's a lawyer; she'll scrutinize data privacy, AI accuracy, and liability)
5. Can I show ROI to my CFO in a business case?

**Who else is involved in the decision:**
- **General Counsel (GC):** Final sign-off. Cares about risk reduction and liability.
- **CFO:** Approves budget. Wants to see hard-dollar ROI (cost avoidance, headcount efficiency).
- **IT/InfoSec:** Security review. Will want SOC 2, data processing agreements, SSO support.
- **End users (attorneys/paralegals):** If they hate the UX during a pilot, the deal dies.

**Typical deal timeline:** 30-60 days from first demo to closed-won, assuming a trigger event is present and no procurement bureaucracy.

### Messaging That Resonates

**What to say:**

| Message | Why It Works |
|---|---|
| "Review 500 contracts in the time it takes your team to do 50" | Directly addresses the volume-to-headcount mismatch she lives every day |
| "Catch the clause your team missed at 6pm on a Friday" | Speaks to her fear of human error under volume pressure |
| "Your team does the strategic work. AI handles the repetitive review." | Repositions the tool as a way to elevate her team, not replace them |
| "See exactly which contracts deviate from your playbook" | She has standards; she needs visibility into deviations at scale |
| "One customer caught a $2.3M auto-renewal before the opt-out window closed" | Quantified pain point that matches her lived experience |

**What NOT to say:**

| Message | Why It Fails |
|---|---|
| "Replace your legal team with AI" | Threatening. She'll shut down immediately. She protects her team. |
| "Enterprise-grade CLM platform" | She doesn't want a platform. She wants a surgical tool that solves one problem fast. |
| "Works for any document type" | Too generic. She wants to know it works on NDAs, MSAs, and vendor agreements specifically. |
| "Schedule a demo to learn more" | She doesn't have time for exploratory demos. Lead with the value, show the product in context. |

### Objections and How to Handle Them

| Objection | What She's Really Saying | Response Strategy |
|---|---|---|
| "How accurate is the AI?" | "I can't afford a false negative on a liability clause" | Share accuracy benchmarks on specific clause types. Offer a paid pilot where she tests on her own contracts. Position AI as a first-pass filter, not a replacement for attorney judgment. |
| "My team won't adopt another tool" | "We've been burned by shelfware before" | Show time-to-value data from similar customers. Offer a 2-week pilot with her actual contracts. Emphasize that the tool fits into existing workflows (email, Drive, SharePoint), not a new portal. |
| "We need to run this through procurement" | "I like it but I don't control the budget process" | Arm her with an ROI calculator and a one-page business case template. Offer to join a call with her CFO or procurement team. |
| "What about data security?" | "I'm sending confidential contracts to a third party" | Lead with SOC 2 Type II, encryption at rest/in transit, data processing agreement. Offer on-prem or VPC deployment if available. |
| "We're looking at [competitor] too" | "Convince me you're better without badmouthing them" | Focus on speed-to-value and contract review depth vs. full CLM platforms. "They're great if you need a full CLM. If you need contract intelligence that's live in two weeks, that's us." |

### Personal Motivations

**Career goals:**
Diana wants to become a General Counsel within 3-5 years. To get there, she needs to demonstrate that she can run legal as a strategic business function, not just a cost center. Implementing AI-powered contract review is a visible, quantifiable win she can point to in her promotion case.

**How she measures her own success:**
- Contract turnaround time (days from request to executed agreement)
- Risk incidents caught before signing
- Team satisfaction and retention
- Perception of legal as a business enabler vs. bottleneck

**What a win looks like for her:**
She presents at the quarterly leadership meeting: "We reduced average contract review time from 5 days to 1.5 days, caught 23 high-risk deviations that would have gone unnoticed, and did it without adding headcount. Here's the tool that made it possible." The CFO nods. The CEO says "great work." Her GC texts her afterward: "Nice job."

# PART 3: ACTIVATION PLAYBOOK

## How to Use This ICP and Persona Operationally

### For Marketing
- **Content strategy:** Write case studies and blog posts that center on compliance scares and missed renewals as the inciting incident. Diana reads peer stories, not product pages.
- **SEO/SEM targets:** "contract review automation," "AI contract analysis," "reduce contract review time," "contract risk detection." Avoid "CLM" as a keyword; that attracts the wrong audience.
- **LinkedIn targeting:** Head of Legal, VP Legal, VP Procurement, Senior Counsel at companies with 200-1,000 employees in SaaS, Fintech, Professional Services.
- **Community presence:** Sponsor or speak at ACC (Association of Corporate Counsel), CLOC (Corporate Legal Operations Consortium), and local GC roundtables.

### For Sales (SDR/BDR)
- **Outbound trigger monitoring:** Set alerts for: companies that just hired a new GC or Head of Legal (LinkedIn job changes), companies in the news for compliance issues, companies that recently raised Series B-D funding (growth = contract volume spike).
- **Opening message framework:** Lead with the trigger event, not the product. "I saw [company] just brought on a new Head of Legal. When leaders step into that role, contract review workflows are usually one of the first things they audit. Curious if that's on your radar."
- **Qualification questions:** (1) How many contracts does your team review per month? (2) Do you have a contract management platform today? (3) What triggered your interest in looking at this now?

### For Sales (AE)
- **Discovery framework:** Map the pain to dollars. "You mentioned a missed renewal cost the company $200K. How many renewals does your team track manually today? What would it mean if you caught 100% of those?"
- **Demo structure:** Don't show the product in abstract. Ask Diana to send 5 real contracts (redacted if needed) and demo the tool on her actual documents. This is the single highest-converting demo motion.
- **Multi-threading:** Diana is the champion but not the sole decision-maker. Ask early: "Who else will need to be comfortable with this before you can move forward?" Get a call with the GC and CFO before the proposal stage.
- **Proposal framing:** Lead with risk avoided and time saved, not features. "Based on your volume of 800 contracts/month, we project a reduction in review time from 5 days to 1.5 days, saving your team approximately 1,200 hours per quarter."

### For Customer Success (Post-Sale)
- **Onboarding success metric:** Time to first value = Diana's team reviews their first 100 contracts through the tool within 14 days of go-live.
- **Expansion signal:** When contract volume increases or new departments start sending contracts through the tool, that's the expansion trigger.
- **Renewal risk signal:** Low usage in months 2-3. If the team stops using it, the renewal is at risk. Intervene immediately with a re-enablement session.
