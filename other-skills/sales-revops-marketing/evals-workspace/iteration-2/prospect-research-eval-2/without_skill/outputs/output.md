## Recommended Approach

**Do NOT rely solely on your existing champion.** Given the reorg risk, you need a dual-track approach:

### Track 1: Champion Insurance
- Reach out to your champion immediately, but frame it as a check-in, not a panic move
- Ask how the transition is going, who's staying, what Marcus's priorities seem to be
- Gauge whether your champion still has access and influence under the new regime

### Track 2: Direct Path to Marcus Webb
- **Channel:** Warm intro (as you mentioned you have one). This is ideal -- a cold outreach to a CRO in his first 90 days has a low hit rate, but a warm intro changes the dynamic entirely.
- **Tone:** Peer-to-peer, consultative. Do NOT pitch. Frame it as: "I've worked with several CROs through transitions like this and I thought it might be useful to compare notes."
- **Timing:** Now. The first 100 days window is open. Every week you wait, Marcus gets more locked into his stack and process decisions.
- **Content:** Lead with the CRO transition insight, not your product. Something like: "When a new CRO comes into a $100M+ org, the first thing they usually need is ground truth on what's actually in the pipeline vs. what the forecast says. That's the exact problem we solve."

### What to Avoid
- Don't mention Sarah Chen or the fact that she was replaced -- let Marcus set that context
- Don't lead with "we're already in your org" -- that can feel like a threat to a new leader who wants to make his own decisions
- Don't rely on your champion to make the intro to Marcus -- use your external warm intro path
