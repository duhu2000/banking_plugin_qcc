### Adapted Pillar-Cluster Model

**Standard model:** 1 pillar per 4-6 weeks, each generating 4-8 derived pieces
**Our adaptation:** 3 pillars across 13 weeks (spaced ~4 weeks apart), each generating 2-4 derived LinkedIn posts

**Tradeoff acknowledged:** The standard pillar-cluster model calls for 4-8 derivative pieces per pillar asset. With a part-time writer also managing event logistics, we are running a reduced cluster of 2-4 derivatives per pillar. This means slower topic coverage and less SEO breadth, but every piece produced will be high-quality, persona-targeted, and CTA-driven -- which matters more for inbound discovery calls than volume.

**Content Repurposing Strategy:**
Each pillar blog post (long-form, 1,200-1,800 words) gets broken into:
1. **LinkedIn post 1:** Key finding or counterintuitive insight from the post (hook + stat + question)
2. **LinkedIn post 2:** Practical framework or checklist extracted from the post (actionable takeaway)
3. **LinkedIn post 3 (optional, capacity permitting):** Short case example or scenario from the post reframed for the alternate persona

This means the writer produces ONE research-heavy asset and gets 3-4 channel outputs from it, maximizing discovery-call-driving content from minimum production effort.

## SMARTER STRUCTURE RECOMMENDATION

Given the constraints, here is what I would recommend as a potentially smarter approach:

1. **Batch production weeks:** Instead of spreading production evenly, batch-write during the pillar weeks. When researching the pillar blog post, draft all 2-3 derivatives in the same sitting. This reduces context-switching and lets the writer handle event logistics on non-production weeks without pressure.

2. **Repurpose aggressively:** Each pillar blog post should be written with LinkedIn extraction in mind. Use subheadings that can become standalone posts. Write key stats as pullable one-liners.

3. **Keep the calendar flexible:** The buffer weeks (9 and 13) are intentional slack. If an event crunch hits in week 6, slide Pillar 2 to week 7 -- the buffer absorbs the delay without breaking the schedule.

4. **Do not add channels until this cadence is proven sustainable for 2 consecutive quarters.** If the team proves it can maintain 1-2 pieces/week for 26 weeks, then consider adding a quarterly webinar or CEO LinkedIn in Q3. Adding channels prematurely is how newsletters die.
