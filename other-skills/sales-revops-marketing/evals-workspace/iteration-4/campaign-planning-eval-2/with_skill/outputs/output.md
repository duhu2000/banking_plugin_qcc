STRATEGY: Thought Leadership-Led

Thought leadership is the correct strategy because we are targeting mid-market SaaS companies (200-2,000 employees, $20M-$200M ARR) with a complex, trust-based buying motion around AI-powered revenue operations. The audience is sophisticated; they respond to intelligence they already want, not product pitches. The brief confirms a benchmark report generated 280 MQLs at $180 CPL -- the best campaign ever run -- proving that research-driven thought leadership is the highest-performing format for this audience.

AUDIENCE DEFINITION

Primary: VP Revenue Operations, Head of RevOps, Director of Sales Operations at B2B SaaS companies with 200-2,000 employees and $20M-$200M ARR. Decision-makers evaluating or already implementing AI in their revenue stack.
Secondary: VP Sales, CRO, and VP Marketing at the same firmographic profile -- these are the budget holders who the RevOps leader must convince.
Size: Estimated 4,000-6,000 mid-market B2B SaaS companies in North America fitting firmographic criteria; ~12,000-18,000 target contacts across primary + secondary personas.
Reach: Target 200,000 impressions across LinkedIn + syndication; 3,000+ content downloads; 400 MQLs.

CHANNEL MIX WITH BUDGET ALLOCATION

Phase 1 (Weeks 1-4): Research Asset Production & Seeding

Original Research Report Production ($12,000)
Purpose: Create the anchor asset -- the 2026 State of AI in Revenue Operations Benchmark Report. This is the campaign's lead magnet and the content pillar from which all derivative content flows.
Format: 20-30 page gated research report with original data (survey of 200+ RevOps leaders), benchmarks, maturity model, and actionable recommendations. Produced by content marketer 1 (lead author) with demand gen specialist managing survey distribution.
Target: Production-complete by end of Week 4; soft launch to email list in Week 5.
Rationale: The historical benchmark report generated 280 MQLs at $180 CPL -- the best-performing campaign ever. This channel earns the largest content production investment because it directly replicates the proven format. The 2026 edition builds on that success with fresh data and an AI-specific angle that matches current market interest. Blog content alone has never moved the needle, so the strategy anchors on gated research, not ungated blog posts.

Email Nurture Setup & List Preparation ($3,000)
Purpose: Build the nurture infrastructure in Salesforce before the research report launches. Segment existing database by firmographic fit; prepare 8-touch nurture sequence.
Format: Salesforce segmentation + Outreach sequence configuration. Content marketer 2 writes nurture email copy (8 emails across 6 weeks).
Target: Nurture sequences tested and ready to activate by Week 5.
Rationale: Email is the highest-ROI B2B channel. Preparing nurture infrastructure in Phase 1 ensures no lead goes un-nurtured once the report launches. Low cost ($3K for tooling/configuration) with highest expected CPL efficiency.

Phase 2 (Weeks 5-8): Launch & Primary Distribution

LinkedIn Sponsored Content ($18,000)
Purpose: Primary paid distribution channel for the research report. ICP-targeted ads driving to gated download page.
Format: LinkedIn Sponsored Content with firmographic targeting (200-2,000 employees, SaaS industry, RevOps/Sales Ops titles). Three ad variants: key finding highlight, data visualization, and contrarian insight. LinkedIn Thought Leader Ads from company executives for 3x CTR uplift.
Target: 150,000 impressions; 0.6% CTR (above 0.40% benchmark due to Thought Leader Ad mix); 900 clicks; 200+ downloads; 60-80 MQLs from LinkedIn alone.
Rationale: LinkedIn is the primary channel where mid-market SaaS RevOps leaders spend professional time. It is the best-fit paid channel for reaching this ICP with firmographic precision. The research report format (not generic blog promotion) gives LinkedIn ads a content asset worth clicking on. Paid search is excluded per brief constraints (brand terms too expensive). This receives the largest paid media allocation ($18K) because it is the most targetable channel available.

Content Syndication ($15,000)
Purpose: Distribute the research report through third-party B2B content syndication networks to reach net-new audiences outside our existing database.
Format: Gated report syndicated via partners (e.g., TechTarget, NetLine, G2) with firmographic and title filters matching ICP. CPL-based pricing.
Target: 120-150 MQLs at $100-$125 CPL (syndication typically runs lower CPL for high-quality gated assets).
Rationale: Content syndication was a core distribution channel for the original benchmark report that generated 280 MQLs. It scales reach beyond our owned audience and LinkedIn targeting. The research report format is ideal for syndication because it offers genuine value to the reader, driving higher conversion rates than product-focused content. Events are excluded per constraints, making syndication the primary net-new reach channel.

Email Nurture Activation ($2,000)
Purpose: Activate the 8-touch nurture sequence for all report downloaders and existing database contacts matching ICP.
Format: 8-email sequence over 6 weeks: (1) Report delivery, (2) Key finding #1, (3) Key finding #2, (4) Webinar invitation, (5) Implementation framework, (6) Case study, (7) Self-assessment tool, (8) Consultation offer.
Target: 35% open rate; 5% click rate; 80+ MQLs from nurtured contacts converting on mid/bottom-funnel offers.
Rationale: Email nurture is the highest-ROI B2B channel and the primary conversion mechanism for moving top-of-funnel report downloaders to MQL status. Minimal incremental cost ($2K for Salesforce send volume and A/B testing) since sequence content was produced in Phase 1. Blog content alone never moved the needle, but blog content repurposed as email nurture content within a structured sequence does convert because it delivers value in a permission-based, progressive format.

Phase 3 (Weeks 6-12): Conversion & Sustained Demand Gen

Webinar Series -- 3 Sessions ($10,000)
Purpose: Mid-funnel conversion event. Each webinar deep-dives into one finding from the research report, featuring a guest RevOps leader from a recognizable mid-market SaaS company.
Format: 45-minute live webinar + on-demand replay. Three sessions spaced across Weeks 6, 9, and 11.
Target: 300 registrants total; 150 attendees; 25-35 MQLs (webinar attendee-to-MQL rate: 15-25%).
Rationale: Webinars are a proven mid-funnel accelerator in B2B SaaS when anchored to original research (not generic topics). The brief's historical failure was "generic webinars" -- these are not generic. Each session is tied to a specific, data-backed finding from the benchmark report and features an external practitioner, which drives both registration and attendance. Budget covers promotion, production, and guest speaker coordination.

LinkedIn Retargeting ($5,000)
Purpose: Re-engage report page visitors who did not download, webinar page visitors who did not register, and partial-form-fill abandoners.
Format: LinkedIn retargeting via Matched Audiences. Ad variants: social proof (download count), alternate finding, and webinar replay CTA.
Target: 0.8% CTR; 40+ additional downloads from retargeted visitors.
Rationale: Retargeting warm visitors is a standard efficiency play that captures intent that was expressed but not converted. Small budget ($5K) because the audience is limited to page visitors. No historical evidence of failure for retargeting; it supplements the proven LinkedIn + research report approach.

Derivative Content Production ($10,000)
Purpose: Fund production of all derivative content from the research report: webinar decks, LinkedIn carousel posts, email nurture content, infographics, and blog summaries.
Format: Content marketer 1: webinar content + decks. Content marketer 2: LinkedIn posts (2/week), email sequences, blog summaries. Designer (half-time): infographics, carousel graphics, webinar visuals.
Target: All derivative content produced on a rolling basis, 1-2 weeks ahead of publication schedule.
Rationale: The research report is the content pillar; derivative content extends its reach across all channels without requiring new original research. This is the content leverage model -- one research investment, many distribution formats. Explicitly avoids standalone blog content (which never moved the needle) by ensuring every blog post is a derivative of the research report with a CTA back to the gated asset.

LEAD MAGNET

2026 State of AI in Revenue Operations Benchmark Report.
A 20-30 page research report based on a survey of 200+ RevOps leaders at mid-market SaaS companies, covering: (1) AI adoption rates in revenue operations by function (forecasting, lead scoring, pipeline management, data hygiene), (2) maturity model -- where mid-market companies sit vs. enterprise, (3) ROI benchmarks -- what AI investments are actually returning in pipeline velocity and forecast accuracy, (4) the "AI readiness gap" -- what RevOps teams need before AI tools deliver value.
Why they want it: RevOps leaders are under pressure to implement AI but lack benchmarks from their peer set. Enterprise data dominates the conversation; mid-market-specific benchmarks are scarce. This report fills that gap with data from their exact peer group.
Format: Gated PDF (ungated executive summary for social sharing). Estimated production: 3 weeks (content marketer 1 leads; demand gen specialist manages survey; designer formats).
