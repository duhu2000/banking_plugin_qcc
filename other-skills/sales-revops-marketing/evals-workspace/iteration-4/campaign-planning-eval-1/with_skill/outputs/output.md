ENTERPRISE ABM EXPANSION CAMPAIGN BRIEF
Goal: Generate $3.4M in qualified expansion pipeline from 6 named accounts | Budget: $120,000 | 90 days (Weeks 1-13)
================================================================

STRATEGY: Account-Based Marketing / ABM

ABM is the correct strategy because we are targeting six named enterprise accounts -- all existing customers -- for platform-wide expansion deals averaging $850K ARR. The motion is hyper-targeted, multi-contact, multi-touch, and account-specific. The brief confirms personalized ABM with custom landing pages worked historically; generic broad-reach tactics did not.

AUDIENCE DEFINITION

Primary: VP/SVP of Technology, CTO, Head of Platform Engineering at JPMorgan, Goldman Sachs, Citi, BNY Mellon, State Street, Northern Trust. Decision-makers responsible for enterprise software expansion and platform consolidation.
Secondary: Director of Procurement / Vendor Management, IT Architecture leads who influence platform decisions.
Size: 6 named accounts; estimated 8-12 key contacts per account = 48-72 target contacts.
Reach: 100% of target contacts engaged via at least two channels within 90 days.

CHANNEL MIX WITH BUDGET ALLOCATION

Phase 1 (Weeks 1-4): Foundation & Account Intelligence

LinkedIn Sponsored Content -- Account-Targeted ($18,000)
Purpose: Warm all 48-72 contacts across 6 accounts with account-specific thought leadership before direct outreach begins.
Format: LinkedIn Sponsored Content with company-list targeting (6 accounts only). Three ad variants per account cluster (investment banks vs. custodian banks).
Target: 80%+ of target contacts reached; 0.5% CTR benchmark.
Rationale: LinkedIn account-targeting is the only paid channel that can precisely reach named financial services executives at specific firms without relying on intent data providers (which legal has prohibited due to EU subsidiary constraints). Personalized ABM landing pages -- the approach that historically worked well -- require a traffic source, and LinkedIn is the best-fit driver for that traffic in this audience. Allocated $18K because it serves as the primary awareness channel across all 13 weeks.

Personalized Landing Pages -- 6 Account-Specific Pages ($15,000)
Purpose: Dedicated microsite page per account showing platform-wide ROI vs. their current mid-market plan usage, customized to each firm's publicly known technology stack and regulatory environment.
Format: Six custom landing pages with account-specific ROI calculators, case studies from comparable financial services deployments, and a clear CTA to schedule an expansion review.
Target: 15%+ conversion rate from page visit to expansion review request.
Rationale: The brief explicitly states that personalized ABM with landing pages has worked well historically. This channel receives significant investment ($15K) because it is the proven conversion mechanism for this motion. Each page is account-specific, not generic -- directly replicating what worked before.

Phase 2 (Weeks 3-10): Multi-Touch Engagement

Executive Direct Mail + Digital Follow-Up ($12,000)
Purpose: Break through digital noise with a physical touchpoint for C-suite and VP-level contacts, followed by a coordinated digital sequence.
Format: Custom-branded executive briefing package (printed ROI analysis + invitation to private strategy session) sent to 2-3 senior contacts per account, followed by LinkedIn InMail and email sequence.
Target: 50%+ open/engagement rate on direct mail; 30%+ response to follow-up sequence.
Rationale: For six named enterprise accounts in financial services, direct mail to senior executives has a higher signal-to-noise ratio than any purely digital channel. This is a net-new tactic not covered by historical failures. Generic webinars -- the approach that did not work -- failed precisely because they lacked personalization; direct mail inviting to a private strategy session is the opposite of generic.

LinkedIn InMail -- 1:1 Personalized ($8,000)
Purpose: High-intent, low-volume direct outreach to engaged contacts who have visited landing pages or interacted with sponsored content.
Format: Personalized InMail from AE assigned to that account, referencing specific page visits or content engagement.
Target: 25%+ response rate (ABM benchmark for warm contacts).
Rationale: InMail is used only for contacts showing engagement signals, making it a high-efficiency channel. It complements the personalized landing page approach that historically worked well by extending the 1:1 conversation. Budget is modest ($8K) because volume is intentionally low -- only 48-72 contacts total.

Email Nurture -- Account-Specific Sequences ($5,000)
Purpose: Owned-channel nurture for all contacts in HubSpot; highest ROI channel for sustained multi-touch engagement.
Format: 6-touch email sequence per account segment (investment bank vs. custodian bank), each referencing account-specific value propositions and linking to personalized landing pages.
Target: 35% open rate; 5% click rate; 10%+ of nurtured contacts request expansion review.
Rationale: Email nurture is the highest-ROI B2B channel and costs only platform fees. These are existing customers, so we have permission-based email addresses already in HubSpot. Low allocation ($5K for content production and HubSpot workflow setup) with high expected return. Directly supports the personalized approach that worked historically.

Phase 3 (Weeks 5-13): Conversion & Pipeline Acceleration

Account-Specific Executive Briefings -- Virtual ($18,000)
Purpose: Private, invitation-only briefings (NOT generic webinars) for 2-3 senior stakeholders per account, presenting custom ROI analysis and platform expansion roadmap.
Format: 45-minute virtual executive briefing per account, led by our executive sponsor + AE, with custom slide deck showing account-specific usage data and expansion value.
Target: 4+ of 6 accounts attend; 75%+ of attendees advance to formal expansion opportunity.
Rationale: These are NOT generic webinars, which historically did not work for this audience. Executive briefings are private, account-specific, and invitation-only -- the opposite of the webinar format that failed. They are the final conversion event in the ABM funnel. Significant allocation ($18K) because they are the highest-intent touchpoint before pipeline creation.

Retargeting -- Landing Page Visitors ($6,000)
Purpose: Re-engage contacts who visited personalized landing pages but did not convert.
Format: Display retargeting via LinkedIn Audience Network, showing account-relevant proof points.
Target: 0.8% CTR; 5+ additional expansion review requests from retargeted visitors.
Rationale: Retargeting warm ABM contacts is a standard efficiency play. Budget is modest ($6K) because the audience is small (landing page visitors only). No historical data suggests this channel has failed; it supplements the proven landing page approach.

Content Production -- ABM Assets ($20,000)
Purpose: Fund creation of all account-specific content: ROI analyses, custom slide decks, landing page copy, direct mail packages, and executive briefing materials.
Format: 6 account-specific ROI analyses, 6 landing pages, 6 briefing decks, 6 direct mail packages, 3 LinkedIn ad variants.
Target: All assets production-complete by Week 4 (landing pages, ads, email sequences) and Week 8 (executive briefing decks).
Rationale: ABM requires account-specific content, which is production-intensive. Allocating $20K to content production ensures the one content marketer can contract supplemental writing/design support to meet quality standards. The personalized approach that worked historically depends entirely on content quality.

HubSpot ABM Tooling & Analytics ($3,000)
Purpose: Configure HubSpot target account dashboards, account scoring, engagement tracking, and AE alert workflows.
Format: HubSpot ABM tier configuration, account-level engagement scoring, automated AE notifications when accounts cross engagement thresholds.
Target: Real-time account engagement visibility for all 4 AEs and marketing team.
Rationale: Operational infrastructure required to execute the campaign. Low cost, high impact on execution speed.

Contingency Reserve ($15,000)
Purpose: Unallocated budget held for reallocation at Week 4 decision gate based on account engagement data.
Rationale: Protects the $120K total from being fully committed before we have performance data. Will be deployed to highest-performing channels at the Week 4 review.

LEAD MAGNET

Custom Platform Expansion ROI Analysis -- per account.
Each of the six accounts receives a bespoke ROI analysis comparing their current mid-market plan utilization against projected value from platform-wide adoption. The analysis includes: (1) current usage metrics and feature adoption gaps, (2) peer benchmarking against comparable financial services firms on the platform, (3) projected ROI from full-platform deployment including compliance, security, and operational efficiency gains, (4) specific cost savings from consolidating point solutions.
Why they want it: These are existing customers -- they already trust the platform. A custom analysis showing concrete dollar value of expansion, benchmarked against their financial services peers, directly addresses the business case they need to build internally for an $850K expansion.
Format: PDF + interactive web version on personalized landing page. Estimated production: 3-4 days per account (one content marketer + shared designer).

Decision gate: If fewer than 3 of 6 accounts have reached MQA status (engagement score >= 50) by Week 4, execute the following:
1. Pause LinkedIn ad spend on non-responding accounts (save $6,000-$9,000)
